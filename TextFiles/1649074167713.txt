: The Labour and Employment Law helps in the understanding of the purpose and function of the labour law, industrial functions, basic conditions of employment, statutory and non-statutory dispute resolution, and resolution of any discrimination of equity in employment.
Labor & Employment Law
LLB degree is offered in this specialisation. Candidates can also pursue LLM. In Employment and Labour Law candidates can learn Human Resource Law, Business Law, Tax Law, etc. The candidates have to also learn about various schemes of employment by the government. The candidates are also taught about the benefits to be provided in the workplace, etc. Learn all about the Labour and Employment Law course here.

Labor & Employment Law Eligibility Criteria
The candidates who wish to study the Labour and Employment law must take admission in college/institute/university. However, to take admission they need to follow certain eligibility criteria which are as follows.
The eligibility criteria for a Labour and Employment Law course may vary from one college to the other. However, candidates are eligible to apply for an integrated course with Labour and Employment Law as a specialisation if they have completed their Higher Secondary School/Intermediate (10+2) or equivalent education from a recognised board.
To pursue course offered in the field of Labour and Employment Law candidates need to possess an degree or equivalent education.

Labour and Employment Law Skillset
Candidates who want to pursue Labour and Employment Law need to be confident and extremely good with written and oral communication. Some key skills that an aspirant should possess in order to be successful in the field of Labour & Employment Law are listed below:

Good at analysing employment policies

Course Curriculum for Labour & Employment Law
The course and the syllabus that is taught under the Labour & Employment law differs from course to course. However, there are some basic areas that are covered under this course. Here are some of the topics listed below:

Industrial relations: Concept and meaning
Collective bargaining – Comparative position in India, USA & UK
Role of ILO in promoting collective bargaining
Need for social security protection of workers
The New Economic Policy and its impact on Management relations in India
Freedom of Association
Concept of Wages
Wage Theories
Need and scope of Minimum Wage
Fair wage and living wage
Fixation of minimum wage Increments
Terms of employment and conditions of service
Prohibition on employment of contract labour – welfare & health of contract labour
Industrial Disputes Act 1947
Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946
The Employees Compensation Act, 1923
The Factories Act, 1948
The Employees State Insurance Act, 1948
The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961
The Unorganized Workers Social Security Act, 2008

Labour & Employment FAQs

Q: What is Labour & Employment Law?
A: It is a law specialisation that
helps in the understanding of the purpose and function of the labour law, industrial functions, basic conditions of employment, statutory and non-statutory dispute resolution, and resolution of any discrimination of equity in employment.

Q: What are Labour & Employment Law courses that can be pursued?
A: Candidates can pursue undergraduate (UG) as well as postgraduate (PG) programmes in the field of Labour & Employment Law. In UG level courses candidates need to pursue integrated law programmes in order to specialise in the field of Labour & Employment Law whereas in a PG level law course aspirants can straight away specialise in Labour & Employment Law.

Q: Are there any Labour & Employment Law course apart from degree courses?
A: Apart from degree programmes, candidates can also pursue diploma courses in the field of Labour & Employment Law. Some of these popular courses are Post Graduate Diploma in Labour & Employment Law, Diploma in Labour Protection and the likes.

Q: What are Labour & Employment Law eligibility criteria?
A: The eligibility criteria for a Labour & Employment Law course may vary from one college to the other. However, candidates are eligible to apply for an integrated course with Labour & Employment Law as a specialisation if they have completed their Higher Secondary School/ Intermediate (10+2) or equivalent education from a recognised board. To pursue course offered in the field of Labour & Employment Law candidates need to possess an degree or equivalent education.
