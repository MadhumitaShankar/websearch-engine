What is UPTET?
Uttar Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test (UPTET) is a state-level exam conducted once a year to enable candidates to gain eligibility for and level teachers in the schools of Uttar Pradesh. The exam is conducted by the Uttar Pradesh Exam Regulatory Authority on behalf of the Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Board (UPBEB). UPTET exam is conducted in two shifts for two papers - Paper 1 and 2. UPTET Paper 1 is conducted for candidates who plan to become teachers of Classes 1-5. On the other hand, UPTET Paper 2 is for candidates who plan to become teachers of Classes 6-8. Candidates who wish to become teachers of Classes 1-8 need to appear in both papers.

Age limit for Paper 1 & Paper 2
Candidates should have attained a minimum of 18 years of age in order to be eligible to apply for the UPTET exam. There is no upper age limit in the UPTET exam.
Educational Qualification for Paper 1 & Paper 2
The education qualification differs for Paper 1 and 2. Have a look at the detailed criteria below:
UPTET Paper 1 Educational Qualification (Primary Teacher)
Candidates need to fulfil any one of the eligibility conditions mentioned below in terms of educational qualification in order to appear for the UPTET Paper 1:

Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent marks and passed or appearing in the final year of 2 years Diploma in Elementary Education.
Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent marks and passed or appearing in the final year of 4 years Bachelor of Elementary Education (BElEd).
Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent marks and passed or appearing in the final year of 2 years Diploma in Education (Special Education).
Graduation and passed or appearing in the final year of 2 years Diploma in Elementary Education.
Graduation with BEd (final year appearing or passed).
UPTET Paper 2 Educational Qualification (Upper Primary Teacher)
Candidates need to fulfil any one of the eligibility criteria given below in terms of educational qualification in order to appear for the UPTET Paper 2:

Graduation with a two-year Diploma in Elementary Education.
Graduation with minimum 50 per cent marks and passed or appearing in one-year BEd.
Graduation with at least 45 per cent marks and a one year BEd.
Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent marks with a four-year BElEd.
Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent marks with a four-year BA/ BScEd/ BAEd/ BScEd.

UPTET Exam 2021-22 FAQs

Q: What is UPTET exam?
A: The Uttar Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test (UPTET) is an eligibility exam conducted to determine the eligibility of candidates as Classes 1 to 8 teachers in schools of Uttar Pradesh. It may be noted here that the UPTET is not a recruitment exam.

Q: When will the UPTET result 2021 release?
A: The UPTET result 2021 will be released soon in April 2022 at updeled.gov.in . The result was earlier set to be released on February 25, 2022, however, the release has been delayed due to the ongoing UP Assembly Elections 2022.

Q: When will the UPTET final answer key 2021 release?
A: The UPTET final answer key 2021 will be released soon. The preliminary UPTET answer key 2021 was released for Paper 1 and Paper 2 on January 27, 2022. The final answer key will release along with the results.

Q: Who can apply for UPTET 2021?
A: Candidates who have cleared the Class 12 exam or completed their Graduation can apply for the UPTET 2021 exam. The eligibility criteria generally differ for Paper 1 and Paper 2.

Q: What is the age limit for the UPTET exam?
A: Candidates should be a minimum of 18 of age in order to be eligible to apply for the UPTET exam. There is no upper age limit for the UPTET examination.

Q: Is UPTET valid for a lifetime?
A: Yes, the UPTET eligibility certificate is now valid for a lifetime. The UPTET certificate was earlier valid for a period of five years. The NCTE extended the validity of TET certificates in 2021.

Q: How many papers does one need to give to qualify the UPTET exam?
A: Candidates planning to teach Classes 1 to 5 need to qualify UPTET Paper 1. Candidates planning to teach Classes 6 to 8 need to qualify for UPTET Paper 2. Candidates planning to teach Classes 1 to 8 need to qualify both papers.

Q: How many attempts can a candidate take in the UPTET exam?
A: There is no limit on the number of attempts in the UPTET exam. Candidates can appear for the UPTET exam numerous times till they attain the desired scores.

Q: Is there any negative marking in the UPTET exam?
A: No, there is no negative marking in the UPTET exam. Candidates are awarded one mark for each correct answer and there is no deduction of marks for incorrect answers.

Q: In which language is the UPTET exam conducted?
A: The UPTET exam is conducted in English and Hindi languages. Candidates need to choose the medium of the exam while applying online for the examination.

Q: How many times is UPTET conducted in a year?
A: The UPTET exam is generally conducted once a year. However, it is solely upon the UPBEB to change the exam frequency depending upon the requirement.

Q: How can I get a job after UPTET?
A: After you qualify the UPTET exam, you are awarded the eligibility certificate. You can apply for teaching jobs in the schools of Uttar Pradesh using the UPTET eligibility certificate. It may be noted that the UPTET exam is only an eligibility exam and does not guarantee a job to the candidate.

Q: How is the UPTET exam centre allotted to a candidate?
A: The UPTET exam centre is allotted to a candidate on the basis of the city preferences mentioned by him/her in the application form. UPBEB does not entertain any request for change in the exam centre after the application process concludes.
