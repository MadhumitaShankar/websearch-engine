Law Job Profiles & Top Recruiters
Law is a career stream that candidates pursue at undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG) or doctorate (PhD) level to practise the legal profession. Law as a profession offers a plethora of career avenues for students to explore and conquer. Following are some of the prominent job descriptions that students can pursue after completing their studies in their chosen field of law:








Law Job Profile


Description



Lawyer

Aspirants for this position are sometimes referred to as advocates. In a courtroom trial, a lawyer usually represents one of the parties/clients. As part of this job description, you'll need to cross-examine witnesses and then write a list of arguments and facts for why the court should rule in your client's favour.



Legal Advisors

One of the responsibilities of this position is to advise clients on their legal rights and obligations. Legal advisors usually conduct research on the laws that apply to a particular issue, then read previous judgments in cases that are similar to the one their client is now facing, and then assist them in determining how they may defend themselves.



Judges

As a judge, you must guarantee that justice is served and that the legal rights of all parties involved are protected. A judge presides over trials and hearings in which he or she must listen to the arguments made by lawyers.



Solicitors

Solicitors provide legal advice and represent clients in court. Solicitors typically work for law firms that specialise in their field of practise.



Legal Executives

People who choose to work in this field often provide legal advice to their companies. They also assist their firm with lawsuit situations. They also handle administrative tasks as well as in-house legal issues like checking deeds, issuing writs, gathering information for affidavits, and drafting legal papers.
Other job profiles one can go for with a law degree are: Notary Public, Oath Commissioners, Trustee, Litigator, Lecturer to name a few.

Q. Which are the top law entrance exam in India?


A. In India, most of the law aspirants go for the following law entrance examinations – CLAT, LSAT, AILET, AIBE and more.



Q. Can I practise law without having a law degree?

A. No, you cannot practise law without obtaining a law degree. In order to practise law in India, you must have a degree such as a BA/BCom/BSc/BBA LLB or LLB.





Q: What is the maximum age limit to pursue LLB?



A.


A: There is usually no upper age limit to apply for LLB courses. The Bar Council of India (BCI) had removed the age criteria for Law admissions a few years back.



Q. What is the ideal time duration of a law programme in Bangalore?



A.


After Class 12, you can follow a five-year LLB programme. If you want to pursue LLB following graduation, it normally takes three years. And, this time duration is not city-specific, it mostly applies to all the law programmes offered in India or abroad.




Q. What educational requirements are required to pursue a career in law?







A. Candidates must have completed Class 12 to enrol in integrated law programmes like BA LLB, BBA LLB, BSc LLB, and BCom LLB. Candidates for three-year LLB degree programmes, on the other hand, must be graduates in any field




Q. What are the different kinds of law courses available in India?







A: Some of India's most popular types of courses are: BA LLB,


BBA LLB, BCom LLB, BSc LLB, LLB and LLM.




Q.



Can any of the law programmes be done via online mode?



A. The majority of reputable institutions and universities offer LLB courses as classroom-based full-time programmes in the offline format. Some universities, on the other hand, now offer some short-term Law programmes via distance and online learning.




Q. Can I practice law once I finish my LLB?


A. After completing the LLB programme, you become a lawyer, but not an advocate. To become an advocate and practise law as a profession, you must pass the All India Bar Examination (AIBE).
