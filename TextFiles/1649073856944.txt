What is ATMA Exam?
AIMS Test for Management Admissions or ATMA is a national-level exam conducted by the Association of Indian Management Schools (AIMS) for admission to , PGDM, and PGDBA courses. More than 750 management institutes across India accept ATMA scores for admission to their management programmes. The test aims to assess the aptitude of candidates, which is necessary for higher management studies. ATMA question paper comprises six sections. The exam is conducted multiple times every year. Questions in the exam are asked from Analytical Reasoning, Quantitative and Verbal Skills.

ATMA 2022: Exam Day Guidelines
Candidates appearing for the ATMA exam must familiarise themselves with exam day guidelines to avoid any sort of difficulty on the day of the exam.

ATMA exam is held from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM. The candidates need to reach the exam venue by 1:00 PM to complete identification and other processes. Latecomers will not be permitted in the exam centre.
The candidates need to carry two printouts of the ATMA admit card with passport size photograph affixed on it and photo ID proof (original and photocopy) issued by the Government of India. They must also carry a printout of the ATMA application form. Candidates not carrying the above-metioned documents will not be allowed to appear for the exam.
Candidates should affix the photograph which matches the application form.
Candidates must have the ArogyaSetu app installed on their phones and the app must show the candidate's risk status. If in case the candidate does not Arogya Setu app, they have to bring the filled self-declaration form. Candidates need to switch off their phones after showing the risk status to the security guard at the centre.
Candidates must bring a ballpoint pen to the centre. Other gadgets such as digital watches, statistical tables, calculators, etc. are not permitted in the exam centre.
The candidate must wear a mask and gloves at all times. They must also carry a transparent water bottle, sanitiser and exam related documents. They must also obey COVID-19 appropriate behaviour at all times.

Important FAQs Related to ATMA 2022

A: ATMA is held four to five times a year. This year ATMA exam will be conducted in the CBT mode. Candidates can appear more than once and the score of the last exam attempted would be considered for admissions.

Q. What is the application fee for ATMA?
A: The application fee for ATMA is INR 1,600 including GST. There is a fee concession for candidates belonging to NE states and women candidates.

Q. Who conducts the ATMA 2022 exam?
A: The ATMA exam is conducted by the Association of Indian Management Schools (AIMS). In 2021, the ATMA exam was conducted in the internet-based test mode across the country due to the coronavirus pandemic.

Q: What is PID number?
A: The PID number or Personal Identification Number is a unique number assigned to the candidate at the time of filling the ATMA application form which can be used by him/her to log in to their ATMA account.

Q. What is the eligibility for the ATMA 2022 exam?
A: Any graduate candidate with 50 per cent aggregate (45 per cent for reserved category) can apply for the exam. The candidates must also be a citizen of India.

Q: Is there any fee concession for reserved category students?
A: Concession in fee will be given to women candidates and those belonging to the North-Eastern states of India (Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura) only. 25 per cent concession will be given to women and 50 per cent concession will be given to candidates belonging to North-Eastern states of India.

Q: Is there any age restriction for the candidates who appear for the ATMA exam?
A: No, there is no age restriction for the candidates who apply for the ATMA exam, however, they must fulfil the other eligibility criteria before appearing for the ATMA exam.

Q: Can I pay the ATMA registration fee by cash/DD or net banking?
A: ATMA registration fee can be paid only through debit/credit cards. There is no provision of paying the application fee offline.

Q. I am unable to upload my signatures in the registration form. What should I do?
A: There is a set limit to upload a photograph and digital signatures in the ATMA registration form. The size of the file should be between 20 KB to 50 KB. The size of the scanned image should not be more than 50 KB. The dimensions of the signatures should be 140 X 60 Pixels.

Q. How many test cities can I choose while filling out the ATMA application form?
Q: Candidates can choose up to two test cities according to their preferences. But in 2021, the candidates were not required to choose test cities as the exam was conducted in the online proctored mode.

Q. I belong to a reserved category. Do I have to fill out a different application form?
A: No, the ATMA application form is the same for all the candidates. They have to choose the category they belong to and provide the supporting documents at the time of admission.

Q. Which documents are needed for uploading while filling the ATMA registration form?
A: Candidates need to upload their passport size photograph, digitally scanned signatures, caste certificate (if applicable), copies of Class 10, Class 12 and graduation mark sheets. Sponsored applicants are required to upload sponsoring certificates as per the format available on the ATMA website.

Q. Can I make corrections in the ATMA application form after submission?
A: No changes can be made once the ATMA application form has been submitted. Candidates must fill the application form carefully and check the details before submitting the form.

Q. Is there any negative marking in ATMA?
A: Yes, 0.25 marks are deducted for every incorrect answer. Candidates will be awarded one mark for every correct answer.

Q. Can I change my test centre after the admit card has been issued?
A: No, candidates cannot change their test centres once the admit card has been issued. The test centre can only be changed if the request has been made at least 10 days prior to the exam and is subject to the availability of seats and approval of test administrator.
