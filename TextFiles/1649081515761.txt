What's happening with BBC Three?




In 2016, BBC Three became an online-only channel. However, we're pleased to announce that from , BBC Three will be returning to screens as a linear channel!
Tuesday 1 February
Broadcasting from 7pm every night, the channel will be available to watch on , Sky, Virgin and Freesat. The live stream will be available on the on BBC iPlayer, with Live Restart available.

What sport will be available on BBC Three?
BBC Three will be hosting live football, with both the semi-finals and final of the (AFCON) being broadcast on launch week. The huge tournament featuring 24 national teams based in Africa is hosted in Cameroon this year, and the three matches all kick off at 7pm. Avid football fans can join the live stream from 6.30pm on match days to join the build-up and enjoy the coverage in full.

Will programmes still be available on-demand?
The majority of our programmes will be available on-demand to stream and download from shortly after the TV broadcast. As with all other channels, there may be times when certain programmes are available to stream-only, have a shorter availability window or may not be available on-demand at all. This will be due to rights agreements.
