Environmental Law Courses
An Environmental Law course is usually offered as a at post-graduation level. However, few universities and colleges also offer the Environmental Law diploma and degree courses at undergraduate level. Apart from that, those interested can also pursue short-term Environmental Law certificate course in online or offline mode. The course duration can range from few months to four-five years, depending on the type of programme in which a candidate has enrolled.

Important FAQs related to Environmental Law

Q. What does an Environmental Lawyer do?
A. An Environmental Lawyer may work on various projects including laws related to climate change, clean technology, water law, management of land, sustainability and so on.

Q. What is the purpose of Environmental Law?
A. The main purpose of Environmental Law is to help protecting the environment as well as the human health.

Q. Is Environmental Law in demand?
A. With the growing public awareness about the importance of environment and environment-friendly procedures, the demand for Environmental Lawyers in India has increased in the past few years.

Q. How much does an Environmental Lawyer earn in India?
A. According to SalaryExpert, the average annual salary of an Environmental Lawyer in India is Rs 11.85 lakh. In addition, most Environmental Lawyers also earn an average bonus of Rs 64,000 per year, as per SalaryExpert.

Q. How can I become an Environmental Lawyer in India?
A. You can follow the below career path in order to become an Environmental Lawyer in India:

Pursue an integrated-LLB course after Class 12 or an course after graduation
LLB
Enroll in in Environmental Law or PG Diploma in Environmental Law course in a reputed law school
LLM
Secure a job as an Environmental Lawyer

Q. What qualifications do I need to become an Environmental Lawyer?
A. You must have a degree or a diploma in Environmental Law from a recognised university in order to become an Environmental Lawyer.
