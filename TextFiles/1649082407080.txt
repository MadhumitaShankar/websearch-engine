Information and Support: Victims of Crime




If you, or someone you know, have been a victim of crime, the following organisations may be able to help.


Victim Support


provides emotional and practical help to victims or witnesses of any crime, whether or not it has been reported to the police.
Victim Support

Phone: 0808 16 89 111 (24/7)

Visit the Victim Support website


Victim Support NI


offers emotional and practical support to all victims and witnesses of crime across Northern Ireland.
Victim Support NI

Phone: 028 9024 3133 (Mon-Fri 9am-5pm)

Visit the Victim Support NI website


Victim Support Scotland


offers emotional and practical support to all victims and witnesses of crime across Scotland.
Victim Support Scotland

Phone: 0800 160 1985 (Mon-Fri 8am-8pm)

Visit the Victim Support Scotland website


Fraud





Action Fraud


provide a central point of contact for information about fraud and financially motivated internet crime. Phone: 0300 123 2040
Action Fraud




Visit the Action Fraud website


UK Safer Internet Centre


provides e-safety tips, advice and resources to help children and young people stay safe on the internet.
UK Safer Internet Centre


Visit the UK Safer Internet Centre website


Get Safe Online


offers unbiased, factual and easy-to-understand information on online safety, including advice for parents about safeguarding children online.
Get Safe Online


Visit the Get Safe Online site


Murder and Manslaughter





Support after Murder and Manslaughter


offers understanding and support to families and friends who have been bereaved as a result of murder or manslaughter, through the mutual support of others who have suffered a similar tragedy.
Support after Murder and Manslaughter

Phone: 0121 472 2912

Visit the Support after Murder and Manslaughter website
