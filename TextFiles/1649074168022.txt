A comparatively recent specialisation, Air & Space Law is known to be a niche and complex subject. Air and Space Law comprises of security and safety laws, environment, air route structures, liability issues, regulation of air services and bi- lateral air agreements in the framework of entirety.
law
A lawyer working in the field of Air and Space Law needs to be well-versed with issues like air environment, safety, security, space tourism, space debris, militarisation of outer space and sub-orbital flights, space medicines, liability and general issues.
One of the primary reasons that Air and Space Law was introduced was due to extensive aviation growth along with commercialisation of space and telecom industries.
Aspirants can pursue postgraduate (PG) programmes in the field of Air & Space Law. Some popular PG level courses in Air and Space Law are – Two Years Master’s Degree in Aviation Law and Air Transport Management, Two Years Master’s Degree in Space and Telecommunications Laws etc.
Apart from these courses, candidates can also pursue Diploma courses in the field of Air and Space Law such as One Year PG Diploma in Aviation Law and Air Transport Management, Post Graduate Diploma in GIS & Remote Sensing Laws, Post Graduate Diploma in Air and Space Law, and the likes.

Required Skillset for Air & Space Law
Aspirants who want to pursue Air & Space Law as a course and career option should be Aviation enthusiasts. Apart from this, candidates in this field should be well-aware of the latest happenings in and around the aviation and space sector. Some key skills that an aspirant should possess in order to be successful in the field of Air & Space Law are listed below:




Interest in information

Knowledge of the aviation and space sector


Good at written communication

Well-aware

Good organisational skills

Fluency

Possess knowledge of technology

Ability to work long hours

Good convincing power

Possess objectivity
Air & Space Law Eligibility Criteria
The eligibility criteria for Air & Space Law course may vary from one college to the other. The eligibility criteria for Air & Space Law course offered at the post-graduation level is such that candidates should have completed a five year integrated law course or possess an / equivalent degree.
LLB
Apart from this, many colleges also specify that candidates should have completed their bachelor’s or equivalent degree in any discipline with minimum 50% marks from a recognised university if they want to pursue a course in Air and Space Law.
Since, Air and Space Law is a niche Law specialisation some colleges also offer admission to candidates if they possess a three year Degree/ Diploma in (AME) along with three years’ industry experience.

Air & Space Law FAQs

Q: What is air and space law?
A: Air & Space Law is known to be a niche and complex subject. Air and Space Law comprises of security and safety laws, environment, air route structures, liability issues, regulation of air services and bi-lateral air agreements in the framework of entirety.

Q: What are courses offered in Air and space law?
A: Aspirants can pursue postgraduate (PG) programmes in the field of Air & Space Law. Some popular PG level courses in Air and Space Law are – Two Years Master’s Degree in Aviation Law and Air Transport Management, Two Years Master’s Degree in Space and Telecommunications Laws etc.

Q: apart from PG courses, what other courses are available in air and space law?
A: Apart from these courses, candidates can also pursue Diploma courses in the field of Air and Space Law such as One Year PG Diploma in Aviation Law and Air Transport Management, Post Graduate Diploma in GIS & Remote Sensing Laws, Post Graduate Diploma in Air and Space Law, and the likes.

Q: What are the Required Skillset for Air & Space Law?
A: Aspirants who want to pursue Air & Space Law as a course and career option should be Aviation enthusiasts. Apart from this, candidates in this field should be well-aware of the latest happenings in and around the aviation and space sectors.

Q: What is the eligibility criteria for air and space law?
A: The eligibility criteria for Air & Space Law course may vary from one college to the other. The eligibility criteria for Air & Space Law course offered at the post-graduation level is such that candidates should have completed a five year integrated law course or possess an / equivalent degree. Apart from this, many colleges also specify that candidates should have completed their bachelor’s or equivalent degree in any discipline with minimum 50% marks from a recognised university if they want to pursue a course in Air and Space Law.
