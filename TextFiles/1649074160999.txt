Business Law is a specialisation which is also referred to as Mercantile Law. Business Law deals with the laws on the basis of which every deal between people and commercial firms are governed.
law
Business Law has two distinct areas of functioning which are:

Regulation of commercial entities that is done through laws of company, partnership, bankruptcy and agency.
Regulation of commercial transactions that are done through the laws of contract.
Aspirants can pursue undergraduate (UG) as well as the postgraduate (PG) level programmes in the field of business law. In UG level courses candidates need to pursue integrated law courses wherein the last few years of the course they can specialise in Business Law whereas in a PG level law course aspirants can straight away specialise in Business Law.
Apart from degree programmes, candidates can also pursue diploma programmes in the field of Business Law. Some of these popular courses are Post Graduate Diploma in Business Laws, Diploma in Business Laws, PG Diploma in Business and Corporate Law etc.

Difference Between Business Law & Corporate Law
Although many people feel that Business Law and are the same, however, it is worth knowing that there are significant differences between the two law specialisations.
Corporate Law
The focus of Corporate Law is majorly on how corporates/ companies are formed as well as managed. Thus, Corporate Law revolves around the operation of a corporation. Corporate law deals with legal rights, obligations and privileges. On the other hand, lawyers working in the field of Business Law cover areas such as contracts, employment laws, commercial transactions and taxes.

The eligibility criteria for a Business Law course may vary from college to college. Candidates who want to apply for a five year integrated course in Business Law meet the eligibility criteria for the course if they have completed their Class 12 or equivalent exam from a recognised with passing marks.

Frequently Asked Questions for Business Law

Q: What is Business Law?
A: The Business Law specialisation deals with all legal aspects required for running of business organisations including laws of copyright, contractual law, workplace safety regulations, financial law, taxation, IPR and more.

Q: What are the subjects of Business Law?
A: The Business Law specialisation is offered through various courses, however, the common subjects to be studied in all the courses are:

Law of Contract/ Commercial Laws
Company Law
Competition Laws
Laws of Arbitration and International Arbitration
Banking Laws
Insurance Laws
Labour Laws
Taxation Laws
Environmental Laws
Human Rights Laws
Mergers and Acquisitions

DFP-Banner

Q: What courses offer Business Law as a specialisation?
A: The business law specialisation is offered as - in Business Laws, Master of Arts in Business Law, in Business Law and Post-Graduate Diploma in Business Laws.
BBA LLB
LLM

Q: What is the difference between Business Law and Commercial Law?
A: The specialisations Business Law and Commercial Laws are interrelated and to make a successful career in either of the specialisations, one has to study both.

Q: What is the scope of Business Law?
A: The candidates studying Business Laws have plenty of opportunities since it is an important part of every business organisation and a graduate in this specialisation has studied all the related subjects. The details about job profiles and recruiters are mentioned above in this article.

Q. How to get admission in Business Law courses?
A. To secure admission in Business Law, candidates should first apply to the university/ law college and then appear and qualify the entrance exam as accepted.

Q. What are some good colleges for Business Law courses?
A. There are many good colleges in India offering courses in Business Law including, NALSAR Hyderabad, NLIU Bhopal, WBNUJS, RGNUL Patiala, School of Law, UPES, SLS Pune, SLS Noida etc.

Q. Do I need to appear in any entrance exam for securing admission in Business Law courses?
A. Yes, candidates need to appear in the entrance exam accepted by the law colleges or universities for admissions to Business Law courses.

Q. How much is the fee for Business Law courses?
A. The course fee for Business Law is anywhere between Rs 5,000 and Rs 11 lakh depending on the courses offered and the colleges.

Q. What are some diploma courses in Business Law?
