A Construction Engineering programme combines aspects of both - Civil Engineering and as well as of Construction Management. A construction engineer is a type of civil engineer who designs, manages and oversees projects within the construction industry. A construction engineer designs, manages and oversees projects within the construction industry. The type of projects they might work on ranges from designing a drainage and sewage system to constructing a building or developing railroads or roadways. The type of construction projects that construction engineers work on can be listed down as under:

- commercial housing or business building
Building
- electrical systems
Electrical
- plumbing, heating or air conditioning
Mechanical
bridges, airports, highways or water waste
Highway or Heavy -
With strong analytical skills, these engineers are good at putting together a good team to finish a project.
One of the major differences between and Construction Engineering is that the former lays more emphasis on design work, thus leading students to a career as design professionals.

Construction Engineering Eligibility Criteria
Interested students can pursue Construction Engineering at the UG as well PG level. The basic eligibility criteria are given below, however, the criteria can be different for every various college.
Students must have passed 10+2 with a minimum of 60% aggregate marks and should have studied Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics as core subjects.
UG level:
Should have completed in Construction Engineering/Civil Engineering with a minimum passing percentage in an aggregate of the subjects studied at the undergraduate level.

Construction Engineering Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q. What is Construction Engineering?
A. Construction Engineering is a professional course where the students learn about the planning and construction of certain structures and infrastructure.

Q. What is the difference between Construction and Civil Engineering?
A. One of the major differences between Construction and Civil Engineering is that the latter is more involved in the planning, designing and implementing projects while Construction Engineering focuses more on the methods, procedures and aspects of construction.

Q. What is the basic eligibility criteria for Construction Engineering admissions?
A. The basic eligibility criteria for Construction Engineering admissions is a 10+2 (or equivalent examination) qualification with a minimum of 60% marks.

Q. What is the course fee for Construction Engineering?
A. The course fee for Construction Engineering ranges from INR 1 - 12 Lakhs.

Q. What are the top job prospects for a Construction Engineering graduate?
A. Some of the top job prospects for a Construction Engineering graduate is Consultant, Construction Planning Engineer, Supervisory Engineer, Resident Construction Engineer and more.

Q. Who are the top recruiters for Construction Engineering?
A. Some of the top recruiters for Construction Engineering are DLF, L&T, Ashoka Buildcon, TATA Consulting Engineers Ltd, Jacobs Engineering and more.
