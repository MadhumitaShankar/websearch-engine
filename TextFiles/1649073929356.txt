NIT Colleges in India: Eligibility Criteria
Engineering courses can be pursued at UG, PG, Diploma, and Ph.D. levels from NITs in India. The eligibility criteria differ as per the course and branch selected by the candidate. However, given below is a broad eligibility criterion for UG, PG, Diploma, and Ph.D. Engineering courses for the reference of candidates:


Candidates must have passed Class 12 from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as core subjects. They must have also secured a minimum of 60 percent aggregate score.
BE/BTech:

Candidates must have passed a three-year Diploma granted by the Board of Technical Examination of a State Government, with a minimum 45 percent aggregate or equivalent.
BTech Lateral Entry:

Candidates who have completed their BE/BTech degree with a valid score in GATE are eligible for the ME/MTech degree course.
ME/MTech:

Aspirants must have passed Class 10/equivalent examination, with at least 35 per cent in Science and Maths subjects.
Diploma:

Aspirants must have secured a minimum of 55 per cent aggregate or 5.5 CPI in BTech/MTech or equivalent degree in relevant streams.
Ph.D.:

Top agencies like NIRF rank colleges as per the parameters such as learning and resources, research, teaching, etc. Candidates can go through these rankings to know the top colleges offering BTech courses. Shiksha rating is given by students based on infrastructure, faculty, course, curriculum, crowd, campus life, and value for money.
