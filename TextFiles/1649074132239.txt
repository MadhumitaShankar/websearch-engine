What is SSC Constable (GD)?
SSC GD Constable exam is a national-level exam conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC). SSC Constable (General Duty) is a popular exam among candidates because of its eligibility criteria, salary and the pride associated with working in the security forces. It is conducted to recruit candidates for the posts of Constable (General Duty) and Rifleman (General Duty) in the following Central Armed Police Forces:



Forces for Recruitment of Constables (General Duty)

Border Security Force (BSF)
Central Industrial Security Force (CISF)

Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF)
Indo Tibetan Border Police (ITBP)

Sashastra Seema Bal (SSB)
National Investigation Agency (NIA)

Secretariat Security Force (SSF)

SSC GD 2022 Selection Process
SSC GD selection process will start with the release of notification. The official SSC GD notification is the first official information about the number of vacancies, exam dates, eligibility criteria, exam pattern, syllabus and other important information. The candidates must read the SSC GD Constable notification to understand all the eligibility conditions given in the official notification.



SSC GD selection process consists of the application process, CBE, PET, PST and medical examination. The complete steps of the selection process are as explained below:
Step 1: SSC GD 2022 Application
The first and foremost step of the recruitment process is filling in the application form. Candidates need to fill out the application form online on the official website of SSC. The application fee for all candidates is INR 100 (women/SC/ST/ex-servicemen candidates are exempted from fee payment).
Step 2: SSC GD admit card
SSC GD Constable admit card is issued one week before the exam. Candidates need to download the SSC GD Constable admit card from the official regional websites of the commission by entering the required login credentials and date of birth. The admit cards for PET/PST and DME/RME are uploaded by CRPF.
Step 3: SSC GD Computer Based Exam
SSC GD CBE was conducted from November 16 to December 15 at the designated exam centres across the nation. The test carries 100 objective-type questions of 100 marks. The question paper is divided into four parts - General Intelligence and Reasoning, General Knowledge and General Awareness, Elementary Mathematics and English/Hindi. The difficulty level of questions is of matriculation level.

Step 4: SSC GD PET/PST
Based on the performance of CBE, candidates are shortlisted for physical tests such as PET and PST. The tests are conducted at various centres finalised by the recruiting body such as CAPFs.
Step 5: SSC GD Medical Exam
Candidates who qualify PET/PST exam then finally need to appear for the Detailed Medical Examination and in some cases Review Medical Examination, conducted by Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs).
Step 6: SSC GD result
SSC GD result is the final step of the selection process. The selected candidates are called to the respective force headquartered for the appointment.

SSC GD 2021-22 FAQs


Q: When will SSC GD answer key be released?
A: SSC Constable GD 2021 answer key (final) has been released on March 28. Check your answers here.

Q: Is the SSC GD 2021 result declared?
A: SSC GD 2021 result has been declared on March 25. Around 2.5 Lakh candidates were declared qualifed for the Physical Efficiency Test (PET)/Physical Standard Test (PST).

Q: What is the qualifying cut off for CBE?
A: The cut-off marks of SSC GD CBE is 30 per cent for General, 25 per cent for SC/ST/OBC/EWS and 20 per cent for ESM categories.

Q: Is the SSC GD 2021 application status released?
A: Yes, SSC GD application form status has been released.

Q: What is the age limit for SSC GD recruitment?
A: Candidates must be 18 years old and should not exceed 23 years.

Q: How can I apply for SSC GD Exam?
A: The candidates could apply for the SSC GD Exam through the official website of the Staff Selection Commission.

Q: What is the salary of the SSC GD Constable?
A: A candidate after selection is entitled to a salary of INR 21,700-69,100.

Q: Is there any negative marking in the SSC GD exam?
A: Yes, there is a negative marking of 0.25 marks for each wrong answer. The SSC GD exam is an objective type exam conducted online as Computer Based Exam (CBE).

Q: Can a 10th pass Apply for SSC GD exam?
A: Yes, the educational qualification required for SSC GD exam is Matric/HighSchool/Class Xth. The candidates appearing in Class Xth are not eligible to apply.

Q: Is SSC GD exam conducted every year?
A: The SSC GD notification is released based on the vacancies intimated by the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) to the commission.

Q: How many vacancies are released for SSC GD exam 2021?
A: The number of vacancies in the SSC GD 2022 exam is 25,271. See here the details .
category-wise and force-wise vacancies of the SSC GD 2022 Exam

Q: What is the application fee for SSC GD exam?
A: Candidates need to pay INR 100 as the application fee for the exam. Women of all categories and SC/ST/Ex-Servicemen candidates are exempted from application fee payment.

Q: What is the minimum eligibility criteria for the SSC GD exam?
A: Candidates should be a Class 10 passed in order to be eligible for SSC GD 2022 exam. The appearing candidates can not apply for the exam.

Q: How many candidates are shortlisted for PET/PST?
A: The number of candidates shortlisted for PET/PST on the basis of merit in CBE is usually 10 times the number of vacancies.

Q: How the final allocation of candidates is done in SSC GD exam?
A: The final allocation of candidates is done based on the performance of candidates in the CBE. PST/PET and medical examination (if applicable) are qualifying in nature.
