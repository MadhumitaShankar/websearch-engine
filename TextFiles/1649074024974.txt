Top Medical Colleges in India Eligibility Criteria


The basic eligibility criteria of the top medical colleges in India are as follows:




Candidates should have passed Class 12 in Science stream (PCB)



They should secure a minimum 50 per cent aggregate or equivalent CGPA




They must have attained the age of 17 years as of December 31 of the admission year




Candidates should be Indian citizens or Overseas Citizens of India (OC) or Parents of India Origin (PIOs)

Top Medical Colleges in India with NIRF Ranking and Shiksha Rating (Aggregate)


Every year, the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF), under the aegis of Ministry of Education, ranks institutes across streams, including Medicine based on multiple parameters such as Teaching, Learning & Resources (TLR), Research and Professional Practice (RP), Graduation Outcomes,(GO), Outreach and Inclusivity (OI) and Peer Perception. The top 100 medical institutes are ranked every year based on these criteria.

FAQs

Q: What are the different specialisations offered in Medical colleges in India?
A: Different specialisations offered in Medical colleges in India are:

Anaesthesia Technology
Perfusion Technology
Public Health
Unani

Q: Which are the best colleges for Medical courses in India based on placements?
A: Top five Medical colleges in India based on Shiksha placement ratings are:

Postgraduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh
Vardhman Mahavir Medical College, Delhi
Sri Ramachandra Institute of Higher Education and Research, Chennai
Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi
Madras Medical College, Chennai

Q: Are there any job opportunities for students who pursue Medical Courses?
A: Yes, there are several job opportunities for Medical students. Some of them are listed below:

Registered Nurse
Medical Researcher
Medical Science Liaison
General Surgeon
Physiotherapist
Anesthesiologist

Q: What is the estimated fee for studying Medical courses in India?
A: It is estimated that pursuing Medical Courses in India is very expensive as 52 per cent of the colleges have annual fees of more than Rs 5 lakh. Some of these colleges are:

Sri Ramachandra Institute of Higher Education and Research, Chennai
St. John’s National Academy of Health Sciences, Bangalore
Kasturba Medical College, Manipal
Siksha 'O' Anusandhan University, Bhubaneswar
Dayanand Medical College and Hospital, Ludhiana
Datta Meghe Institute of Medical Sciences, Wardha
M.S. Ramaiah Medical College, Bangalore
SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Ramapuram
Annamalai University, Tamil Nadu
K.S. Hegde Medical Academy, Mangalore
Mahatma Gandhi Medical College and Research Institute, Sri Balaji Vidyapeeth, Pondicherry

Q: Which are the popular exams to qualify for Medical courses in India?
A: Various exams accepted in India for Medical courses include NEET, NEET PG, AP EAMCET, SAAT, etc.

Q: Is NEET compulsory for studying MBBS?
A:

Yes, NEET is the biggest and only medical entrance exam that acts as the gateway for MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BUMS, BVSc & AH and BSc (Hons) Nursing programmes.

Q: Is NEET mandatory for Nursing, Pharmacy, and BPT programme admissions?
A:

No, NEET is not mandatory for Nursing, Pharmacy and BPT programmes as the medical colleges offering these courses conduct or accept other entrance exams. But NEET scores are also accepted by such colleges and candidates who have a valid NEET score and want to pursue one of these courses, can apply through NEET scores.

Q: Which Medical college in India has the lowest fees?



A:



AIIMS, JIPMER, Lady Hardinge, and PGIMER are amongst the list of colleges that have the lowest fees for pursuing an MBBS in India.
