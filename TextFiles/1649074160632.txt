Edy








Also known as corporate law, enterprise law or business law, company law is a legislation that is concerned with the creation, governance and operation of companies and the related rules, regulations, laws and practices.
Simply put, company law addresses all of the legal issues that are faced by a company. Someone who practices company law is the official legal face of the company and looks after all the cases and suits someone files against the company or the company needs to file against.
Company law aids the companies in complying with various regulations in the business environment and ideally should increase the profitability of a company by following the right taxation procedures, avail the tax benefits and maintain the dignity and the reputation of a company.
In short, corporate law is not private law but is a component of public law.

Company Law Syllabus
A candidate needs to have a fair idea about all of the following if they aim to be a successful corporate lawyer:

The Companies Act, 1956
The Emblems & Names (prevention of improper use) Act, 1950
Industries Development and Regulation Act, 1951
The Securities Contract Regulation Act, 1956
Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992
Depositories Act, 1996
Syllabus of corporate law covers

Formation and structure of companies
Powers of the company and its stakeholders
Corporate governance
Company transactions, corporate finance,
Laws governing securities and security holders
Accounting and auditing
Company meetings, resolutions and transactions
Board of directors – structure, qualification and disqualification, duties of the director
Corporate restructuring, mergers and acquisition
Liquidating the company.
These are only a general description of the course covered. All the rules, regulations, laws, practices and liabilities under each of them will have to be studied.

Important FAQs regarding Company Law

Q. What is Company Law?
A. Company Law includes the set of laws that governs the formation and operation of companies and corporations.

Q. Is Company Law a good career in India?
A. Yes, Company Law can be a rewarding and profitable career path. With different companies being established every now and then, there is good scope in the career.

Q. What is the salary of a Company lawyer in India?
A. A Company or Corporate lawyer has an annual average salary of over Rs 7 lakh, as per PayScale.

Q. What is the eligibility required to pursue Company Law?
A. To pursue Company Law, you can enroll into an integrated-LLB course right after passing Class 12 in any stream. You can also pursue a three-year LLB course after graduation from any stream. Thereafter, you can also pursue LLM in Company Law.

Q. What kind of acts do students have to study in a Company Law course?
A. Students have to study the following acts as part of a Company Law course:

The Companies Act, 1956
The Emblems & Names (prevention of improper use) Act, 1950
Industries Development and Regulation Act, 1951
The Securities Contract Regulation Act, 1956
Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992
Depositories Act, 1996

Q. What subjects are taught in a Company Law course?
A. In a Company Law course, students are taught subjects such as:

Formation and structure of companies
Corporate governance
Accounting and auditing
Corporate restructuring
Corporate mergers

Q. What kind of job roles are offered to Company lawyers?
A. After completion of the course, the Company lawyers are offered job roles such as:

Legal Advisor
Associate Advocate
Legal Executive
Company Secretary
Advocate
Legal Manager

Q. What are the daily job responsibilities of a Company lawyer?
A. Some of the daily job responsibilities of a Company lawyer include:

Preparing legal documents
Assessing business partnerships
Negotiating deals
Representing the company in legal proceedings

Q. What skills are important to be a successful Company lawyer?
A. Some of the must-have skills for Company lawyers include:

Strong written and verbal communication skills
Strong analytical skills
Good attention to detail
Good managerial skills
Excellent negotiation skills

Q. How can I become a Company lawyer?
A. To become a Company lawyer, you can follow the below-mentioned career path:

Enroll in an integrated-LLB course after Class 12
Enroll in an LLM or PG Diploma Company Law course
Secure a job as a Company lawyer or start your own practice

Q. Which are the popular Law entrance exams that I can take to pursue a career in Company Law?
A. You can appear for top Law entrance exams such as:


CLAT

AILET

LSAT India

DU LLB

TS LAWCET

AP LAWCET
