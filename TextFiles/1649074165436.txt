Energy Law is a specialisation that governs the use as well as taxation of renewable and non-renewable energy. Energy Laws include case law, rules, regulations, statutes and edicts that are related to energy.
law
Further, it is worth knowing that Energy Law deals with creating, enforcing as well as challenging laws that regulate the use of energy. So, Energy Laws exist so that energy creation and harvesting can be regulated. These laws also regulate the taxation of energy use.
The areas of energy regulation include nuclear power, development of clean energy, regulation of oil and gas etc.
Candidates can pursue undergraduate (UG) as well as postgraduate (PG) level programmes in the field of Energy Law. In UG level courses candidates need to pursue integrated law courses which have Energy Law as a specialisation. On the other hand, in a PG level law course aspirants can straight away specialise in Energy Law. Some popular PG level Energy Law courses are LLM in Energy Law, MA in Business Law with specialisation in Energy Law, etc.
Apart from degree programmes, candidates can also pursue diploma and certificate courses in the field of Energy Law.

Required Skillset for Energy Law
Candidates who want to pursue Energy Law need to possess the knowledge and technical skills related to various energy resources. Some key skills that an aspirant should possess in order to be successful in the field of Energy Law are listed below:




Technical knowledge of Energy resources

Interest in detail


Interest in technology

Ability to assimilate as well as analyse facts


Good judge of situation/people

Good writing ability


Mental and physical stamina

Convincing power


Ability to argue on a topic

Integrity


Intellect

Fluency and clarity of speech


Interest in research

Good presentation skills


Confidence

Unbiasedness




Energy Law Eligibility Criteria
Candidates are informed that the eligibility criteria for Energy Law course may vary from one college to the other. However, candidates are eligible to apply for an integrated Energy Law course offered at a college/ university of their choice if they have completed their Higher Secondary School/ Intermediate (10+2) or equivalent education from a recognised board.
To pursue course offered in the field of Energy Law candidates need to possess or equivalent degree.

Energy Law FAQs

Q: What is an energy law course?
A: Energy Law is a specialisation that governs the use as well as taxation of renewable and non-renewable energy. Energy Laws include case law, rules, regulations, statutes and edicts that are related to energy.
law

Q: Can energy law courses be pursued at UG and PG levels both?
A: Yes. Candidates can pursue energy law courses at both UG and PG levels.

Q: Apart from the degree courses, are there are any other courses in Energy Law?
A: Apart from degree programmes, candidates can also pursue diploma and certificate courses in the field of Energy Law.

Q: What does an Energy lawyer do?
A: An energy Lawyer deals with creating, enforcing as well as challenging laws that regulate the use of energy. So, Energy Laws exist so that energy creation and harvesting can be regulated. These laws also regulate the taxation of energy use.

Q: What is an energy law course?
A: Energy Law is a specialisation that governs the use as well as taxation of renewable and non-renewable energy. Energy Laws include case law, rules, regulations, statutes and edicts that are related to energy.
law

Q: Is it worth taking energy law?
A: Yes, the energy sector is constantly growing and there is a constant demand for law and regulation enforcement in this sector and hence, energy law is worth taking up as a course.

Q: What are the areas included in energy regulations?
A: The areas of energy regulation include nuclear power, development of clean energy, regulation of oil and gas etc.

Q: What are the degrees in the energy law courses?
A: BA LLB (Hons) in Energy Laws is taught at the UG level. At PG level candidates can pursue MA in Business Law with specialization in Energy Law, LLM in Energy Law, etc.

Q: Why do we need energy laws?
A: Energy Laws exist so that energy creation and harvesting can be regulated. These laws also regulate the taxation of energy use.

Q: What is a popular PG course in Energy Law?
A: Some popular PG level Energy Law courses are LLM in Energy Law, MA in Business Law with specialisation in Energy Law, etc

Q: What is a popular UG course in Energy law?
A: Popular UG course in Energy law is BA LLB Energy Law.
