About RRB Group D 2022 Recruitment
RRB Group D is a national-level exam conducted by the RRBs on behalf of the Railway Recruitment Cells (RRC). RRB Group D recruitment is a good opportunity for candidates who wish to join the Indian Railways. The recruitment is conducted to select candidates for the various posts in Level-1 of the seventh CPC pay matrix such as Track Maintainer Grade-IV, Helper/Assistant in various technical departments (Electrical, Mechanical, and S&T departments), Assistant Pointsman, and many more.
Against 1,03,769 vacancies, over 1.15 crore candidates had registered for RRC Group D 2022 exam.

RRB Group D 2022 New Changes
Some of new changes announced in RRB Group D 2022 recruitment process are given below:

Ministry of Railways has decided to include issues of Course Completed Act Apprentices (CCAAs) trained in Railways
The Ministry of Railways has decided that CCAAs trained in the railway establishments will be given due weightage for the training done by them in Railways Establishments in preparation of the final merit list after the conduct of CBT for RRB Group D.
CCAAs will be exempted from Physical Efficiency Test
CEN RRC-01/2019 (Level-1) will be a single stage exam. There will be no second stage CBT
RRC wise CBT will be held for Level-1. Also maximum available capacity is recommended to be used, to significantly reduce the number of shifts involved for each RRC and to speed up the examination process
Percentile based normalization will be used wherever the number of shifts involved are more than one
Medical standards as prescribed in Indian Railway Medical Manual (IRMM) will be used for different posts of Level-1
Any available Income and Asset Certificate for candidates, who have applied under Economically Weaker Sections (EWS), will be considered as Valid
The designation of Assistant Pointsman has been changed to Pointsman

It has been decided to follow revised methodology with special conditions for conduct of CBT for Level-1 as there is no CBT 2 exam
The methodology will involve mobilising additional infrastructure and logistics to conduct CBT for Level-1 with significant increase in per shift requirement
Efforts will be made to have the exam conducting agency (ECA) on board to conduct the CBT for Level-1 as early as possible

RRB Group D 2022 FAQs

Q: When RRB Group D exam will be conducted?
A: RRB Group D 2022 exam will be conducted from July 2022.

Q: Why high power committee was formed for RRB NTPC exam?
A: High power committee was formed by the Ministry of Railways to examine the concerns submitted by candidates against the introduction of second stage CBT exam in RRB Group D.

Q: Is RRB Group D exam pattern and selection process revised?
A: Yes, RRB Group D exam pattern and selection process have been revised. It has been decided to follow revised methodology with special conditions for conduct of CBT for Level-1 due to elimination of 2nd stage CBT etc. This will involve mobilising of additional infrastructure and logistics to conduct CBT for Level-1 with significant increase in per shift requirement. Efforts will be made to have the exam conducting agency (ECA) on board to conduct the CBT for Level-1 as early as possible.

Q: Where will RRBs release the admit card for RRC Group D exam?
A: RRB Group D admit card for CBT 1 will be released on the regional official websites four days before the exam. Candidates can download their admit cards by logging with the required credentials from this page too.

Q: How many vacancies are announced for RRC Group D exam?
A: RRBs have announced 1,03,769 vacancies for RRB Group D exam. A total of 1,15,67,248 application forms have been received against the vacancies.

Q: How many exam stages are there for RRB Group D?
A:
The exam stages of RRB Group comprises
CBTs (First Stage and Second Stage), Physical Efficiency Test, Document Verification, and Medical examination. However, the examination authority reserves the right to conduct CBT in a single-stage or multi-stage.

Q: Is there any qualifying marks in RRB Group D exam?
A: Yes, there are qualifying marks in RRB Group D exam. The qualifying marks for UR and EWS categories are 40 and 30 for
OBC (Non-Creamy Layer),
SC, and ST.

Q: What is the job profile for RRB Group D?
A: The recruitment of RRB Group D includes various technical and non-technical posts. The job profiles differ as per the
hierarchy level of different railway departments. Candidates who join RRB Group D based on their performances are promoted to higher posts.
Also, candidates become eligible for departmental examinations after three years of service.

Q: What is the difference between RRB Group D and RRB NTPC?
A: RRB Group D is a level-1 job while RRB NTPC (non-technical popular categories) is a level 2 to 7 job. The basic salary of candidates selected through RRB Group D is INR 18,000.

Q. What is the medical test in the RRB Group D exam?
A. After document verification in RRB Group D exam, candidates are called for a medical test. It is mandatory to clear the medical test to be considered for the recruitment exam. The medical fitness test is conducted by the railway administration to ensure that the candidates are medically fit to carry out the duties connected with the post.

Q. What is the physical efficiency test in the RRB Group D exam?
A. Once candidates qualify for the computer-based tests of RRB Group D exam, then they are called for Physical Efficiency. Passing Physical Efficiency Test (PET) is mandatory, and it is qualifying in nature.

Q: What is RRB Group D qualification?
A: Candidates who had completed Class 10 or had National Apprenticeship Certificate (NAC) provided by NCVT were eligible to apply for RRB Group D exam.

Q: What are RRB Group D posts?
A: RRB Group D exam is conducted for various posts in Level-1 of the seventh CPC pay matrix such as Assistant (Workshop), Assistant Pointsman, Track Maintainer Grade IV and many more.

Q: How to crack the RRB Group D exam?
A: To crack the RRB Group D exam, candidates must make a list of the most important topics by analysing the previous years' question papers. Try to clear the concepts of each and every topic rather than cramming. Set a time to go over the chapters again. Memorise the formulas and definitions. Differentiate weal areas and work to improve them. Solve puzzles to improve logical skills.

Q. How the empanelling of candidates is done in RRB Group D exam?
A: Empanelling of candidates in RRB Group D exam is done based on their performance in CBT, subject to their qualifying in PET. The appointment is offered to selected candidates provided they qualify the requisite medical fitness test and document verification.
