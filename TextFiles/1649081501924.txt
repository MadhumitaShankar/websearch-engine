What is the parental guidance lock?




The parental guidance lock restricts access to programmes which display a to ensure younger audiences can’t access inappropriate content. Setting up a parental guidance lock is the best way to control what your children have access to on BBC iPlayer.
guidance
warning
If a programme has been flagged with guidance, it will feature a clear label and a ‘’ symbol.
G


On your mobile or tablet device, it’ll be a red bar at the top of an episode image.


The parental guidance lock uses a four-digit PIN to control access to guidance-labelled content. When the parental guidance lock is turned on, you'll be asked to set a PIN. You'll need to enter this PIN to view guidance-labelled content.
What are guidance labels?

Guidance labels let viewers know (e.g. violence, sex, drug use or strong language) or (e.g. containing flashing images). Guidance labelling is usually used for television programmes that were broadcast after the TV watershed (9pm).
when content may be unsuitable for younger audiences
when it may be harmful to some viewers
If your child has signed in to their own children's account, they'll see a different version of the BBC iPlayer homepage. This means your child will only see programmes and images that are suitable for them on the BBC iPlayer homepage.
