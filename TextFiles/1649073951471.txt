Eligibility Criteria for Transportation Engineering
For admissions into the Transportation Engineering degree, the students will be required to meet certain eligibility criteria. Along with performing well in the entrance examination, the students will also have to satisfy the requirements, which may vary from institute to institute.

For UG admissions:

Students should have completed a 10+2th standard or equivalent examination from a recognized board.
Should have secured a minimum of 50% in the 10+2th standard qualifying exam.
Students should have studied Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as their core subjects during the 10+2th standard.

For PG admissions:

Should have graduated with the minimum required marks from a recognized institute
Students should have a BTech Civil Engineering or any other related degree.
Required Skillset for Transportation Engineering
While meeting the eligibility requirements is necessary for admissions into a Transportation Engineering degree, the students should also have some skillsets to excel in their academics and career. If the students have the mentioned skillsets, then they will be able to do well.

Good communication skills
Analytical and logical reasoning skills
Interpretation of data
Problem-solving skills
Creativity
Teamwork
Leadership skills
Mathematical knowledge

Transportation Engineering Syllabus
Students who opt for BTech Transportation Engineering will have to complete the course within four years (BTech) and two years (MTech). Various Transportation Engineering subjects have to be studied during the course to successfully obtain the degree.
Topics under BTech Transportation Engineering
Since BTech Transportation Engineering is under Civil Engineering, the following topics will be covered during the four years

Transportation Planning
Building Technology
Transportation systems
Traffic and pavement engineering lab

Geometric Design of Transportation Engineering

Introduction, Design of at grade intersections, Design of interchanges, Geometric design of facilities for non-motorized traffic, Introduction to Geometric design of airfields, Design of drainage facilities.

Transportation System Planning and Management

General, Transportation Systems, Urban Transportation Planning Studies, Planning Methodology and Systems analysis

RDBMS Civil Engg. Software Packages, Fundamentals of optimization, Statistical optimization, Linear Programming, Dynamic Programming, Finite difference methods, Taylor’s series, Fourier series, Different Implicit and Explicit schemes - MacCormack Scheme, Lambada Scheme, Preissmann Scheme, Stability analysis, Boundary Conditions, Algebra of tensors


Design of Experiments & Research Methodology

Basic principles of design of experiment, Error analysis in experiments, Concept of modelling, Different types of models, Random variables, Random numbers, and Analysis of variance, Estimation of parameters, Residual analysis and model checking, Sample size problem, Different types of distributions, Distribution tests, Concept of simulation, Monte Carlo simulation, Geostatistics, Que model, Time series analysis, Fitting statistics, Research Methodology, Statistical and sensitive analysis of data, Interpretation of result and report writing.


Pavement Management System

Introduction, Pavement conditions survey and ratings, Pavement performance prediction, Highway Development and Management tools, Alternate pavement design, Reliability concepts, Road asset management, Expert systems and pavement management.


Land Use & Regional Transportation Planning

Urbanisation; urban forms and structures, Delineation of regions, Landuse transportation model, Transit-oriented land use planning, Regional and intercity travel demand estimation, Freight travel demand modelling, Regional network planning, Policy formulation and evaluation.


Transportation Economics and Finance

An economic evaluation of transport plans, Vehicle operating costs, Value of travel time savings, Accident costs, Traffic congestion, traffic restraints and road pricing, Highway finance


Transportation and Environment

Introduction, Vehicular emissions, EIA studies of highways and ambient air quality standard, Noise measurements, noise levels and noise control, Introduction to sustainable Transportation infrastructure


Mass Transportation Systems

Introduction to various types of the mass transportation system, Urban mass transportation planning, Terminals and their functions, Operational and management issues in transit planning.


Intelligent Transportation Systems

Advanced traffic management systems, advanced traveller information systems, Commercial vehicle operations, Advanced public transportation systems, Advanced rural transportation systems, advanced vehicle control systems, ITS standards; ITS technologies and the future of ITS.


Airport Infrastructure, Planning and Design

Introduction, Airport Planning and Geometric Design, Planning and design of the terminal area, Structural design of airport pavements, Airside Capacity and delay, Air traffic control, Airport drainage.


Advanced Soil Engineering

Origin, nature and distribution of soils, Description of individual particle, Consolidation, Factors affecting strength, Geosynthetics, Methods of site investigations, Slope Stability analysis.


Advanced Engineering Geology

Mineralogy and Geomechanics, Geomorphology, Engineering Problems.


Pavement Analysis and Design

Fundamentals of pavement design, Analysis and Design of Flexible pavements, Analysis and Design of rigid pavements, Rehabilitation and Maintenance of Pavements, Pavement evaluation and overlay design.


Railway Infrastructure, Planning and Design

Rail transportation, Railway track gauges and Geometric design of the track, Track maintenance including modern method, Railway accidents and Disaster Management, Suburban railways in Metro cities.


Highway Materials, Design and Construction

Soil sub-grade, soil stabilization and low-cost road, Desirable characteristics of Road aggregates, proportioning of aggregates, Types of bitumen, bitumen Constituents, characterization of bitumen, emulsions, modified bitumen, Desirable properties of bitumen mixes and design of bitumen mixes, tests on Bitumen, theory of filler and specification, Factors influencing and design of pavement grade concrete mixes, Bituminous and concrete road construction Procedure and specification, Quality assurance


Bridge Engineering

General Bridge system, Loading Standards, Design of Bridges, Bridge Bearings, Design of substructure - Piers and Abutments, Dynamic Response of Bridges, Limit State concept for Design of RCC bridges.


Advanced Foundation Engineering

Shallow Foundations, Deep Foundations, Soil structure interaction, Soil Liquefaction and remedial measures, stone column, vibrfloatation, deep compaction, Foundations in difficult soils.

Transportation Engineering Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Is Transportation Engineering a good job?

A. Currently, there is high demand for candidates in Transportation Engineering. There are many opportunities in the private and government sectors for students who complete their Transportation Engineering.

Q. Is Transportation Engineering a part of Civil Engineering?

A. Yes. Transportation Engineering is a branch of Civil Engineering. However, during Masters, the students will be able to study Transportation Engineering as a separate course.

Q. What is the average salary of Transportation Engineering graduates?

A. The average salary of Transportation Engineering graduates range from 4 lakhs to 15 lakhs per annum. The salary depends on the experience of the employee as well as the organization that they are working for.

Q. Which are the top entrance exams for Transportation Engineering?

A. The top entrance exams for Transportation Engineering are JEE Main, JEE Advanced, VITEEE, MHT CET. For PG admissions, GATE is the most popular entrance examination.

Q. Which are the top recruiters for Transportation Engineering?

A. There are many government and private sectors, which offer jobs to Transportation Engineering graduates. Some of the top recruiters are Jacobs, CDM Smith, Alstom Transport, Ashoka Buildcon and more.
