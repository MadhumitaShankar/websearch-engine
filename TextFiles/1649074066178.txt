Reaching career heights is the ultimate aim of every ambitious professional and Master of Design is one of the degrees that helps in specialisation and advancing skills for a career in Design field. Pursuing a master degree in Design is possible only if you have completed a bachelor degree in relevant stream. The two-year degree offers to learn of real-world based techniques, industry-specific theories and design skills through projects so that one learns nitty-gritty to the core.
A Master of Design degree is the gateway to specialise and research as it an advanced degree to conquer Fashion and Design Industry in different disciplines such as Craft, Textiles, Special Needs, Publications, Media, Lifestyle Products, Graphics and User Experience Design. The degree is open to students from various streams such as Fashion, Designing, Fine Arts, Engineering, Commerce, Science and Architecture. However, you should have a basic understanding of relevant design stream which you want to pursue through Master of Design.




The scope of Master of Design degree is huge as it opens opportunities in various streams such as Industrial Engineering and Design, Product Research Analyst, Communication Design, Product Design and Engineering, Visual Design, Animation and Automotive Designing.

Required Skillset for Master of Design



Candidates who want to pursue Master of Design should have a basic understanding of design tools, techniques and applications. But besides all these essential skills, they should have skills for M. Des as mentioned below –




Forecasting and Analytical skills

Communication, persuasive and people networking skills


Project Planning and Management

Creativity and Innovation


Observational skills and eye for details

Computer design software i.e. CAD/AutoCAD/CAM


Leadership and Management skills

Client Handling



Master of Design Eligibility Criteria



Candidates should hold a 4 year Bachelor degree/Diploma in Design stream with relevant subjects. However, there is no strict criteria for the relevant subject. Moreover, candidates holding a degree in Architecture and Engineering or an equivalent 4-year degree can also pursue a Master in Design course in relevant specialisation.






Course curriculum for Master of Design



The Master of Design course is offered in various specialisation. The course curriculum may differ for every design institute. The four-semester degree offers design subjects at an advanced level and not basic knowledge based subjects.
