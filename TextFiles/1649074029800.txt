Master of Physiotherapy offers a solution to joint-related issues, movement problems caused by a wide variety of nerve, muscle, and joint disorders. It teaches a person to stay fit and the importance of the human body in their life. It will help in avoiding injuries and take precautions from an accident or joint-related problems. Physiotherapy is the study of preventing injuries, finding drug-free techniques and helping people to always stay fit. Students can choose from a variety of specialisation fields such as sports-related physiotherapy, neurological conditions, movement disorders, rehabilitation, and recovery. Overall, the students will learn about the complete human body and the best practices to keep them fit.

This is one of the emerging professions in ‘Fit India’ and will offer ample opportunity over the years. The course follows a semester system in general, with a dissertation in the last year. If you have an interest in health science and aspire to be a physiotherapist, then the salaries offered in the sector are handsome.

Courses in Master in Physiotherapy and Specializations

Several institutes and universities offer postgraduate and doctorate programs in physiotherapy with a good number of placements. A student gains enough experience enabling them to work independently in improving muscle strength, bone strength, recovery of body parts, and operations, etc.

Masters in Physiotherapy offers an opportunity to experience practical learning and to gain required skills to be an expert physiotherapist.

Generally, Master of Physiotherapy has following specialization courses in the universities across India:

MPT Specializations:


Masters in Physiotherapy in Cardio-pulmonary Sciences

Masters in Physiotherapy in Community Physiotherapy

Masters in Physiotherapy in Neurosciences

Masters in Physiotherapy in Orthopedics

Masters in Physiotherapy in Pediatrics and Women Health





Difference between Bachelor, Post Graduate and Doctorate degree


If a candidate wants to be a general trainer or physiotherapist trainee, then BPT (Bachelor’s Physiotherapy) will cover all the general physio subjects.

MPT (Masters in Physiotherapy) includes one specialized subject and detailed analysis about the same with research published articles and internships of at least six months.

Doctorate degrees are mandatory for applying to the post of a lecturer.s.

Eligibility Criteria for MPT courses

The essential eligibility criteria for Masters in Physiotherapy in India.


Completed 10+2 with Biology stream, with a desire to solve physical problems of humans and have an excellent knowledge of Physiotherapy and relative subjects.

Candidate must be qualified for a bachelor’s degree from the recognized universities having a 50% more aggregate score in it and six months of internship experience in the same field.

A candidate can apply under the various categories General/ OBC/ SC/ ST; NRI students have the same opportunity with NRI categories in the Foreign/NRI category.

A student may apply for the same masters course before an internship. However, admission is guaranteed only after completion of an internship. One has to be a graduate from reputed colleges or universities affiliated with the Indian Physiotherapy Association for better enhancement and salary package.

Career/Scope

Physiotherapy offers an ocean of opportunities in emerging India. It is one of the best choices nowadays in terms of cost, money, and reputation amongst others. You could be the reason for someone overcoming his/her physical disability, or be a helping hand for people who face various challenges in their day-to-day life. Physiotherapy is a basic need in every sector, from sports to normal human life. One can get opportunities to serve our soldiers, sportsmen or in one of the leading hospital chains.

If a candidate has an interest in research, then he can be a guide for junior students. You can be involved with research-based analysis of different age groups in India and do a health survey. Besides, one can teach in universities to improve the physical healthcare unit and spread awareness about the same. You can also work as a physiotherapist, sports therapist, acupuncturist, exercise physiologist, and various other roles in any medical organization.

Along with India, the demand for physiotherapists is increasing exponentially in foreign countries, too. The western countries always encourage physiotherapist jobs with attractive pay packages for both freshers and experienced professionals. In physiotherapy, one gets not only money but also goodwill from patients and their families. An excellent opportunity to spread awareness about physical health and the importance of physique in one’s life.

MPT: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
Q. How important is the MPT degree for a physiotherapist?
A. MPT (Masters in Physiotherapy) is very important. Basic-entry level jobs are also hiring physiotherapists with a master's degree. It is thus important for those who wish to take a job.
Q. What is MPT?
A. MPT is the abbreviation for Masters in Physiotherapy. It is the postgraduate degree awarded for a program or course in the area of physical therapy or physiotherapy.
Q. Which are the best colleges for MPT in India?

A. Some of the most popular college for MPT in India are:

M.S. Ramaiah Medical College, Bengaluru
AWH Special College, Kozhikode
Jaipur National University
Sri Aurobindo Institute of Medical Sciences, Indore
Nizam's Institute Of Medical Sciences, Hyderabad
Q.What is the admission process for MPT in India?
A. A candidate who has a Bachelor's in Physiotherapy (BPT) and has completed six months of compulsory internship can apply for MPT. Most colleges in India admit candidates on the of the marks obtained in the qualifying examination of BPT. Candidate, however, needs to keep a track of the application forms of the desired institute.
Q. What is the average salary of someone who has completed MPT?
A. The starting salary of a Physiotherapist with an MPT degree can be anywhere between 3 Lac to 5 Lac. It may increase up to 12 lac with 5-10 years of experience.
