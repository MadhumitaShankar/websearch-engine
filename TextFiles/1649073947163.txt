Electronic Control System Engineering is a specialized discipline of engineering which deals with the impact of electrons, needed to make equipments, devices, and systems. It utilises automatic control theory to design systems which yield desired behaviours in controlled environments. The job of the Electronic Controlled System Engineers is to resolve various issues related to digital control systems. These engineers are capable of analyzing, designing, and optimizing complex systems in order to receive the desired effect within a controlled environment. Hence, the Electronics Controlled System Engineering can be described as an amalgamation of coordination of mechanical, electrical, chemical, metallurgical, electronic or pneumatic elements.

Electronics Control System Engineering Course
There are a handful of colleges that conduct undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in Electronic Control System Engineering. The undergraduate course is for 4 years while the postgraduate programme is for 2 years. Like, other engineering courses, the undergraduate programme can be pursued after completing 10+2 Board examination and the postgraduate programme can be pursued after completing the undergraduate programme. Eligibility Criteria to Pursue Electronics Control System Engineering
The candidates who wish to pursue undergraduate course in Electronics Control System Engineering would require to fulfill certain criteria to be eligible for the course. To pursue undergraduate course they would have to sit for the entrance tests like or the other state level Entrance tests. Besides, they should have completed 10+2 Board examination along with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as subjects. Those who have appeared for their Class XII Board examination are also eligible to appear for the entrance test.
JEE Main
To pursue the postgraduate course in Electronics Control System Engineering, the candidates would need to sit for and to complete the undergraduate programme in the same discipline or in Electronics Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Electrical and Communication Engineering, and Electrical and Instrumentation Engineering. Those who are in the final semester of their undergraduate programme are also eligible to write the examination.
GATE

Electronics Control System Engineering Course Structure and Fees
We have already discussed that the duration of B. Tech course is of 4 years and that of M. Tech course is of 2 years. An academic year comprises of two semesters; odd and even. The odd semesters usually begin in July/ August and the even semesters begin in January/ February. An examination is held at the end of each semester.
The fee for the undergraduate courses varies from college to college. However, to pursue the B. Tech programme in Electronics Control System Engineering the candidates would have to pay a fee as low as Rs. 14,000 in a year and as high as Rs. 3 lakhs in a year.

Job Profile of Electronics Control System Engineers
There are ample job opportunities for Electronics Control System Engineers. They can work at pure-play automation companies, and oil and gas sectors. At the completion of their courses, these engineers may accept jobs both in the private sectors and government sectors. They are absorbed by the companies in various fields such as: Remote Sensing, Textile and Chemical Industries, Agricultural-based industries, Aeronautical areas, and in the manufacturing units. Initially, these engineers earn Rs 3-4 lakhs per annum. But their income increases with years as they accumulate experience.
