What is SBI PO exam?
State Bank of India (SBI) every year conducts . A large number of candidates apply for the exam, out of which only a few are selected depending on the number of vacancies. SBI PO is considered one of the most sought-after banking exams in the country. Successful candidates are posted as Probationary Officers in various branches of the bank. SBI PO exam comprises three phases-Prelims, Mains, and Interview (Or Interview & Group Exercises). SBI is a prestigious organisation that offers handsome salaries and job security to its employees as a result of which a large number of candidates rush to apply for the various exams conducted by the bank.
SBI PO exam to select eligible candidates for the post of Probationary Officer
Approximately 10 lakh candidates apply for SBI PO exam every year.

SBI PO Highlights
Check below for some important highlights related to the SBI PO exam.




Exam Particulars

Exam Details


Exam Name

SBI PO


Conducting Body

State Bank of India


Exam Level

National level


Exam Frequency

Once a year


Mode of Exam

Online


Total Registrations

Approximately 10 lakh


Stages of Exam

Prelims, Mains and Interview


Exam Fees


INR 750 for General/EWS/OBC categories

SC/ST/PWD categories are exempted from fee payment


Exam Duration

Prelims: One hour Mains: 3 hours 30 minutes



No. of Papers and Total Marks

Prelims: Three (100 marks) Mains: Four (250 marks)



Total Questions

Prelims: 100 questions Mains: 156 questions



Marking Scheme

0.25 marks are deducted for wrong answers


Language/Medium of Exam

English and Hindi


Exam Purpose

For the selection of Probationary Officers in various branches of SBI


No. of Test Cities

Prelims: 200 cities (Approx.)
Mains: 70 cities (Approx.)

SBI PO FAQs 2021

Q: When SBI PO 2021 final result will be announced?
A: SBI PO 2021 final result has been announced on March 15.

Q: When SBI PO 2021 mains result was announced?
A: SBI PO 2021 mains result was announced on January 25, 2022.

Q. What is the age limit for applying to SBI PO exam?
A. The age limit for applying to SBI PO is between 21 and 30 years. However, relaxation in the upper age limit is provided to candidates belonging to the reserved categories.

Q. I have completed graduation from the Science stream. Can I apply for SBI PO?
A. Yes, candidates from any discipline can apply for the SBI PO exam.

Q. I am in the final year of graduation. Can I apply for SBI PO exam?
A.Yes, candidates can apply for SBI PO exam provisionally. However, at the time of the document verification, candidates are required to produce their degree certificates.

Q: Which one is better Bank PO or MBA?
A. MBA is not a career, it is a Postgraduation course that may prove beneficial to your career. Probationary Officer or PO, on the other hand, is a career. Candidates clearing SBI PO exam are recruited in any of the branches of SBI as PO.

Q. Do graduation marks matter in SBI PO exam?
A. There is no minimum percentage of marks in graduation prescribed for applying to SBI PO.

Q. How many times can I give SBI PO exam?
A. There is capping on the number of attempts in SBI PO exam. General Category candidates can take four attempts. For other categories, the number of attempts is limited to seven. Candidates within the prescribed age limit can apply for the exam.

Q. Is there any bond in SBI PO?
A. The selected candidates, at the time of joining, are required to execute a bond of Rs 2 lakh to serve the bank for a minimum period of three years.

Q. Will the selected SBI PO candidates be on probation?
A. Yes, the selected SBI PO candidates are on probation for two years.

Q. What is the career growth of SBI PO?
A. Candidates can be promoted from the Middle Management Grade Scale-II (MMGS-II) to Junior Management Grade Scale-I (JMGS-I) based on performance.

Q: How is the final appointment of candidates done through SBI PO exam?
A: The final appointment of candidates through SBI PO exam is done based on the mains exam and group exercises and interview.
