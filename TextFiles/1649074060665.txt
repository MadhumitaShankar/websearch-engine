Film and video design is a creative field which is concerned with the creation as well as integration of films and videos. Over a period of past few years, film and video design as a course has gained immense recognition. A bachelor’s or master’s degree in film and video design gives students all the essential skills and knowledge required to embark on a career in designing and producing content for films, television, games, etc.
In a film and video design course, candidates are taught various subjects like , videography, , film language, , production design etc. Given below is everything you need to know about film and video courses in terms of eligibility criteria, course curriculum, job prospects and more.

Eligibility Criteria For Film & Video Design
Film and video design courses are offered at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Given below is the basic eligibility criteria for both levels of courses:
: Candidates should clear Class 12th in any stream from a recognised board with at least 50% - 60% aggregate marks.
UG Courses
: Candidates should clear graduation in any discipline with minimum 50% - 60% aggregate marks.
PG Courses
It may be noted that film and video design eligibility criteria mentioned above is basic and might vary from institute to institute.
Course Curriculum for Film & Video Design
There are many film and video design courses offered by various higher education institutions. Among all courses, the two most popular courses include BDes Film & Video Design and MDes Film & Video Design. Given below is course curriculum for both the courses:
: The curriculum mentioned in the tables below include both theoretical as well as practical (project/ internship) subjects taught to students.

Film & Video Design: Job Profiles & Top Recruiters
Graduates or postgraduates in film and video design can build their careers in the broad spectrum of moving image industry including TV channels, feature films, advertising agencies, production houses, government agencies, corporate firms, etc. Some of the popular job profiles for film and video design students include:

: A production designer creates and manages visual aspects of a film, television or theatre production. The professional works in coordination with the director and producer to create a design style for aspects such as graphics, sets, lighting, etc.
Production Designer
: A cinematographer is a professional who manages camera and lighting crews and oversees selection as well as operation of technical equipment to create images. A cinematographer is basically responsible for the visual impact created by a film.
Cinematographer
: An art director is responsible for overall visual aspects of a media or advertising campaign. Some of the daily responsibilities of an art director include coordinating the work of other design staff such as graphic designers and hiring as well as supervising assistant art directors.
Art Director
: A video editor edits and assembles recorded raw material (such as camera footage, dialogue, special effects etc) into a suitable and finished product ready for broadcasting.
Video Editor
: A filmmaker usually refers to the director or producer of a film. A filmmaker is a person who is responsible for taking a film from the development stage to the finished product.

FAQs on Film & Video Design

Q. What is film and video communication?

A. Film and Video Communication is that sphere of design in which individuals are trained to understand various elements of videos and film making. As part of Film and Video Communication course, aspirants are taught subjects like videography, photography, film making, production design, cinematography, and the likes.

Q. What does a title designer do?
A. A Title Designer is someone who is responsible for creating the opening titles sequence and title cards for a television show or film. A title designer is also responsible for creating end credits for the film/video piece.

Q. What is a designer’s salary?
A. The salary offered to a designer varies depending on the project they are working for as well as the company for which they work. As per payscale.com, on an average film and video designers earn between INR 25,000 to INR 75,000 per month.

Q. Who can pursue Film and Video Design?
A. Aspirants who have a creative flair as well as knowledge of design and video software are apt to pursue a course in Film and Video Design. Candidates can pursue Film and Video Design course at both UG and PG level. To study Film and Video Design at the UG level, aspirants should have completed their 10+2 from a recognised board with minimum 50% aggregate marks. On the other hand, PG level course can be pursued by candidates if they possess a graduation degree in a similar field from a recognised college with minimum 50% aggregate marks.

Q. What jobs can I join after doing Film and Video Design?
A. After completing a course in Film and Video Design, candidates can pursue any of the below mentioned job profiles:

Production Designer
Cinematographer
Art Director
Video Editor
Filmmaker
