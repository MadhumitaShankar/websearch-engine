Administrative Law deals with the study of procedures, rules, and regulations of any organisation. There are laws that govern the operation and rules of any organisation. These laws if broken can come under criminal offences. Hence the study of such law is called Administrative Law.
As per the official website of National Law School of India University,
“Administrative Law is primarily a judge-made law and the course will primarily deal with the study of different principles of Administrative Law which have been evolved by the courts to prevent arbitrary use of power and protect the rights of the citizens.”

Administrative Law Eligibility Criteria
To admission to law colleges to pursue Administrative Law, candidates have to check the eligibility of the course. The eligibility might differ from college to college. Hence the candidates must go through the required age limit, qualifications and all other details carefully.
Some basic eligibility is given below:

To pursue a UG course in Administrative Law, candidates must pass 10+2 with minimum qualifying marks as set by the institution.
For admission in a PG course, candidates must have an LLB degree. If a candidate has pursued a 5-year integrated law degree, they can also apply for PG Administrative Law course.

Skillset for Administrative Law
A lawyer needs a basic skillset to ace in this profession. Administrative Law requires some additional skills like attention to detail, good observation, sharp memory, inquisitiveness, decision making, and more. Some of the most important skill sets are as follows:
- A good Constitutional Law lawyer should have excellent communication skills, which can help in boosting his career.
Communication Skills
- A lawyer’s task is not just to represent his/her clients in court but also to present different facts and figures in the court. Sharp memory will help in presenting points in stating them with confidence.
Sharp Memory
- A lawyer should not just have a positive approach but also have a problem-solving attitude to gain the trust of his/her clients.
Problem Solving Attitude

Q: What is Administrative Law?
A:
Administrative Law deals with the study of procedures, rules, and regulations of any organisation.

Q: What are the law degrees offered in Administrative Law?

BA LLB (Honours)


BBA LLB (Honours)

LLM

PG Diploma

Q: What is the eligibility for pursuing Administrative Law?
A:
To pursue a UG course in Administrative Law, candidates must pass 10+2 with minimum qualifying marks as set by the institution.
For admission in a PG course, candidates must have an LLB degree. If a candidate has pursued a 5-year integrated law degree, they can also apply for PG Administrative Law course.

Q: Can a 10+2 pass pursue Administrative Law?
A: Yes they can.

Q: Is there any NLU offering Administrative Law courses?
A: Yes, NLU Bhopal and GNLU, Gandhinagar offer Administrative Law courses.

Q: What are some entrance exams that I can give for admission to the Administrative Law courses?
A: You can appear for CLAT, AILET, ILI CAT, LSAT.

Q: What are some job profiles of Administrative Law?
A: Some of the job profiles are:


Legal administrator

Administrative Lawyer

Administrative judge

Legal writer

Counsellor for Agency leaders

Q: Is Administrative Law a good career option?
A: Yes, it is. Administrative Law is considered as most prestigious legal job particularly those that involve criminal law, appellate law, administrative or regulatory law, civil rights law, government relations law etc.

Q: Can I become an Administrative Lawyer without a law degree?
A: No, a law degree is a must for a career in Administrative Law.

Q: What is the maximum duration of a law course?
