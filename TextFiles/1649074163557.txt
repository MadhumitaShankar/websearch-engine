Banking Law is a specialisation in the field of law, which deals with legal aspects of the functioning of banks and banking services. The world of finance and economy is dynamic. The government often brings about regulatory changes in practices and activities in the financial sector which has direct bearing on banking. Thus, it is important for a country to have banking laws, which help in regulating the banking activities and prevent irregularities.
After completing a course in Banking Law aspirants become a legal advisor or start own legal practice for law firms, accounting firms, banks, international corporations and other business organisations.
One can pursue Banking Law at Undergraduate (UG) as well as Postgraduate (PG) level as part of courses. Some of the top law schools in India include National Law School, Nalsar University of Law, Faculty of Law-Jamia Milia Islamia, Faculty of Law-DU, Aligarh Muslim University, Symbiosis Law School, Allahabad University, etc.

Banking Law is a specialisation in Law programmes, thus aspirants need to fulfill the basic eligibility criteria. Given below are the basic eligibility criteria for Banking Law UG and PG courses.
: Higher Secondary School/Intermediate (10+2) or its equivalent exam from a recognised board with not less than 45 per cent marks in aggregate.
UG Courses
: Candidates must have bachelor’s degree or equivalent from a recognised university. For LLM course, candidates must have cleared or equivalent degree from a recognised university.

Banking Law FAQs

Q: What is Banking Law?
A: Banking Law is a specialisation that candidates can pursue at the UG as well as PG level. Banking Law is a niche law specialisation that involves study of laws related to banking, finance, taxation and capital market, etc.

Q: What career opportunities are available after completing the UG course in Banking Law?
A: After completing a course in Banking Law aspirants can start independent practice as a lawyer or become a legal advisor for international law firms, accounting firms, banks, international corporations and large business houses.

Q: What are the courses available in Banking Law?
A: Aspirants can pursue both undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) programmes in Banking Law. It is offered as a specialisation in Law degree programmes.

Q: What are UG courses in Banking Law?
A: At UG level, candidates can pursue LLB, BA LLB and other integrated courses in which Banking Law is taught as a specialisation.

Q: What are the PG courses in Banking Law?
A: At PG level, aspirants can pursue Banking Law as a specialisation in the degree/diploma course. Post the PG course, they can also opt for Doctorate.

Q: What is the eligibility criteria for Banking Law?
A: The eligibility criteria for a Banking Law course at UG level is class 12 or equivalent pass from a recognised board or university. For PG level, the eligibility criteria is graduation, LLB or equivalent degree from a recognised university.

Q: Other than the degree courses, which courses are available in Banking Law?
A: Candidates can pursue short-term certificate or diploma courses in Banking Law apart from the degree courses.

Q: What are the job profiles in Banking Law?
A: After completing a PG course in Banking Law, one can start independent practice or join a firm as corporate lawyer, legal advisor, legal executive, etc.

Q: What is the syllabus of a Banking Law course?
A: Banking Law syllabus includes subjects like Business Mathematics, Torts & Consumer Protection Act, Marketing Management, Financial Market Regulation, Taxation Law, etc.

Q: What are the skills needed for a career in Banking Law?
A: Some key skills that an aspirant should possess in order to be successful in the field of Banking Law are communication skills, convincing power, eye for detail, debating skills, organisational skills, keen observation, analytical skills, etc.

Q: What does a Banking Lawyer do?
A: A banking lawyer gives legal advice to clients with respect to investment and revenue. He/she also works on corporate and finance related legal cases for the clients. Apart from these, a banking lawyer also helps in arranging loans for clients for business acquisitions and mergers.
