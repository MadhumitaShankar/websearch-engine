Best Law Colleges in India Eligibility Criteria
The eligibility criteria for Law programmes vary depending on the course-level. Given below is the basic eligibility criteria that candidates need to fulfill for admission to UG and PG Law courses.

UG Law Courses Eligibility (Integrated-LLB)

Candidates must pass Class 12 in any stream with minimum 45 per cent aggregate
There is no age bar

UG Law Courses Eligibility (LLB)

Candidates must clear graduation in any stream with minimum 45-50 per cent aggregate
There is no age bar

PG Law Courses Eligibility (LLM)

Candidates must possess an LLB degree
There is no age bar

Best Law Schools in India with NIRF Ranking & Shiksha Rating
is a government agency, which releases rankings for top colleges in different streams every year. The rankings released by NIRF are based on different parameters such as Teaching, Learning and Resources (TLR), Research and Professional Practice (RP), Outreach and Inclusivity (OI), Graduation Outcomes (GO) and Perception (PR). On the other hand, Shiksha rating for colleges is given by students and based on different parameters such as placements, infrastructure, faculty and curriculum.

FAQs

Q. Which college is the best for BA LLB in India?
A. As per NIRF Rankings 2020, NLSIU Bangalore followed by NLU Delhi and NALSAR Hyderabad are the best colleges for BA LLB in India.

Q. Which Law college in India has the best placement?
A. NLSIU Bangalore followed by GNLU Gandhinagar and NLU Delhi have the best placements for Law graduates passing from their colleges.

Q. Which state is the best for Law in India?
A. Uttar Pradesh has the maximum number of top ranked Law colleges in the state, including NLU Lucknow, AMU and BHU. Therefore, the state can be termed as the best for pursuing Law from a top ranked Law college.

Q. Is studying Law from a top ranked college in India affordable?
A. Studying Law from a top ranked college is a bit expensive as majority of them have a fee structure of over Rs 5 lakh.

Q. Which exams are accepted by NLUs for Law admission in India?
A. There are a total of 23 NLUs in India. Among these 22 NLUs accept CLAT scores and the one remaining NLU (NLU Delhi) accepts AILET scores for admission.

Q. Which Law entrance exam is required for admission to SIU Pune?
A. For Law admission to SIU Pune, you need to take the SLAT exam.

Q. Which top ranked colleges are the most affordable to pursue Law in India?
A. Faculty of Law, Jamia Millia Islamia (ranked 9 in NIRF Ranking 2020) and Aligarh Muslim University (ranked 13 in NIRF Ranking 2020) are the most affordable best Law schools in India. Both the universities have a fee structure of less than Rs 1 lakh.

Q. Are there any job opportunities for students who pursue Law courses in India?
A. Yes, there are several job opportunities for Law students. Some of them are listed below:

Lawyer
Legal Advisors
Judges
Solicitors
Legal Executives
Notary Public
Oath Commissioner

Q. Which are the top law companies in India that hire law graduates?
A. Top companies that hire graduates from best law colleges in India are listed below:

Amarchand & Mangaldas & Suresh A Shroff & Co.
AZB & Partners
Khaitan & CO
J Sagar Associates
Trilegal
S&R Associates

Q. Can I pursue Law from a top ranked college in India after graduation?
A. About five colleges out of all the best Law schools in India, as per NIRF Rankings 2020, offer the LLB course to students after graduation. Such colleges include:

IIT Kharagpur
SIU Pune
AMU

Department of Law, Panjab University
BHU

Q. What is the eligibility for pursuing BA LLB from a top Law college in India?
A. To pursue BA LLB from a best Law school in India, candidates should clear Class 12 in any stream with minimum 45-50 per cent aggregate. There is no age bar to apply for the course.
