BA LLB (Bachelor of Arts - Bachelor of Legislative Law) is a five-year integrated LLB course that candidates are eligible to pursue after completing Class 12. The is an amalgamation of Humanities and Law streams, which allows candidates to study subjects such as History, Sociology, Administrative Law, Criminology, Family Law and so on.
Law course
The majority of Law schools run a semester system in which the five-year duration course is divided into 10 semesters. The BA LLB course fee in the top law colleges in India ranges from Rs 1.5-2.8 lakh per annum.

BA LLB Eligibility & Skills
Candidates seeking admission to the BA LLB course should fulfil the criteria mentioned below:

Candidates should clear Class 12 in any stream from a recognised school
Candidates should clear Class 12 with a minimum 45-50 per cent aggregate
There is no age bar to pursue the course
: Given above is the basic BA LLB eligibility that might vary depending on the college in which a candidate is applying.
Note

BA LLB Subjects and Syllabus

FAQs regarding BA LLB

Q. What is BA LLB?

A. BA LLB or Bachelor of Legislative Law is an undergraduate degree, which focuses on integrating knowledge regarding law and other subjects to the candidates. Students are also introduced to law case studies, which will help them practice for their career in the law field.

Q. Is BA LLB better or LLB?

A. BA LLB is an integrated 5-year programme, where the student has to study LLB along with other Arts subjects. However, LLB is a 3-year programme that focuses solely on the study of law.

Q. I have a Science background in 12th standard. Can I apply for BA LLB?

A. Yes. Students of any stream can apply for BA LLBprovided that they meet the basic eligibility criteria.


Q. Is BA LLB merit or entrance based?

A. For BA LLB admissions, the students will have to attempt the respective entrance examination of the institute that they wish to apply for admissions.

Q. What are the entrance exams for BA LLB?

A. Some of the national level entrance examinations for BA LLB are CLAT and LSAT. There are many state-level entrance examinations too which the candidates can apply for based on their eligibility.

Q. What is the scope of BA LLB?

A. Students who graduate with a BA LLB degree can start working in the law field. They can work as a lawyer, advocates, solicitors and more. They can also pursue higher education such as LLM or even MBA.

Q. What is the salary of BA LLB graduates?

A. The salary of BA LLB graduates will differ as per the field they are working in or the company or organization that they are working at. However, the average salary falls between INR 3 to 6 Lakhs per annum.

Q. Is CLAT necessary for BA LLB?

A. Yes. CLAT is necessary for BA LLB admissions.

Q. Who are the top recruiters for BA LLB graduates?

A. There are many companies that recruit BA LLB graduates; however, some of the top recruiters are Amarchand & Mangaldas & Suresh A Shroff & Co., J Sagar Associates, Trilegal and more.

Q. Do I need to study Political Science in BA LLB?

A. Yes. Students will also be required to study Political Science along with some other Arts subjects in BA LLB.

Q. Which is the best college for BA LLB?

A. According to India Today Rankings, the National Law School of India University is the top-ranked college for BA LLB.
