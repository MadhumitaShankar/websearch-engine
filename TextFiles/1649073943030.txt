Nanotechnology is the study of controlling matter on a nuclear, atomic and supramolecular scale. The field of nanotechnology is very diverse that includes new methodologies dependent on sub-atomic self-gathering, control of the issue on a nuclear scale, and advancement of new materials with measurements on the Nanoscale. The study of Nanotechnology involves the utilization of various fields of science. Such fields include organic chemistry, microfabrication, molecular biology, atomic science, surface science, and semiconductor material science. The ones who mean to have a career where they get the opportunity to leap forward in advancement in the field of science need to look no further as nanotechnology is the eventual fate of science.

Nanotechnology: Available Courses
The study of Nanotechnology is further diversified into the below-enlisted degrees:

BE (Bachelors of Engineering) Nanotechnology
BTech (Bachelors in Technology) Nanotechnology
MSc (Masters in Science) Nanotechnology
M.Tech in Material Science and Nanotechnology
M.Tech in Nanotechnology
M.Tech in Nanotechnology and Nanomaterials
Doctor of Philosophy in Nanotechnology
Doctor of Philosophy in Nanoscience
An individual who is interested in nanotechnology can pick any of the above-mentioned courses. Candidates with a scientific bent of mind and a strong interest in research work are fit for these courses. These courses are intended for the applicants who are honoured with sound diagnostic and PC programming abilities. Because of the serious research work associated with these courses, it is ideal that the students are prepared to work hard on research.

Required Skillset for Nanotechnology
The study of Nanotechnology looks into the latest trends and developments in various fields of nanotechnology and related skill requirements. It examines the latest development and market trends at the national and international levels. The analysis typically digs into basic skillsets, specifically innovative skills involved in the study of controlling matter on a nuclear, atomic and supramolecular scale.
Skills required in nanotechnology can be differentiated into general and specific. Generic skills mean interdisciplinary cross-sectoral knowledge in natural sciences and engineering as well as basic business management skills. Specific skillsets for nanotechnology involves mastering a certain Nanotechnological procedure.
Enlisted below are the basis of the skill for the competency they fall in.

Knowledge – Professional Competency
Having sound knowledge of different technical branches and natural science fields such as Physics and Chemistry and their special branches for example Photonics, precision optics, laser technology or electroplating.
Knowledge of protection of health and safety standards, instructions, data processing and foreign languages.

Skills & Faculties – Methodological Competency

FAQs

Q. What is nanotechnology?
A. Nanotechnology is a branch of science and technology that deals with the manipulation of matter on atomic, molecular and supramolecular scales. In other words, nanotechnology can be referred to as the study of science and technology that prepares the students for developing nanoparticles and nanomaterials.

Q. Who is more suitable to pursue a career in nanotechnology?
A. The field of nanotechnology is very diverse and suitable to those students who are drawn towards the strategies of manipulation of matter at a nuclear scale. Those who want to have a career where they can find the opportunity to make advancements in the field of science need to look no further as nanotechnology is the eventual fate of science. However, one should also know that this field requires serious research work.

Q. What are the upcoming trends in nanotechnology?
A. Nanotechnology is leaving a deep impact on the growth of technology and making human life easier. After witnessing considerable growth, it can be expected that the field is going to be commercialized in the near future as it helps improve the quality of materials and reduce the risk of environments. In the next few years, we can also see nanotechnology producing renewable materials.

Q. What are the job profiles available in nanotechnology?
A. On completion of a degree in nanotechnology, one can apply for the following job profiles: Project assistant, Business Development Manager, Project Engineer, Service Engineer, Account Executive, Lecturer and more.

Q. How much can I earn in the nanotechnology field?
A. Candidates with a degree in nanotechnology receive jobs at handsome salary packages in India. The average salary offered to a fresher may differ from profile to profile. However, in the beginning, the average salary offered to candidates is between .
INR 2 LPA and INR 5 LPA

Q. What are the employment areas for Nanotechnology?
A. Candidates holding a degree in nanotechnology can work in any of the following areas:

Electronics/semiconductor industry
Forensics
Medical fields and pharmaceuticals
Military and national security
Auto and aerospace industries
Food science including quality control and packaging
Biotechnology

Q. Do I need to have completed bachelors in nanotechnology for admission to M.Sc in nanotechnology?
A. No, candidates who have passed a bachelor’s degree course in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics and Life Sciences subjects can apply for admission to MSc in Nanotechnology.

Q. What are the top institutes for studying nanotechnology in India?

However, only a few per cent of colleges are considered to be good for studying nanotechnology, some good institutes are IIT Madras, IIT Roorkee, VIT Vellore, Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Hyderabad, SRM University, Tamilnadu, Amrita Centres for Nanosciences, Kerala etc.
