IBPS Clerk 2022 Exam Date
IBPS Clerk 2022 exam dates have been announced. . The mains exam will be conducted on October 8, 2022. The prelims exam will be conducted by IBPS only if CET is not held by NRA.
The prelims exam of IBPS Clerk 2022 will be conducted on August 28, September 3 and 9

What is IBPS Clerk Recruitment?
Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) conducts a Common Recruitment Process (CRP) every year to select candidates for the clerical cadre posts in 11 public sector banks. IBPS Clerk exam is conducted in two stages-prelims and mains. Candidates qualifying in both these exams are selected for the Clerical cadre post.
In 2020-21, a total of 7.41 lakh candidates had registered for the exam.

IBPS Clerk 2021 FAQs

Q: When IBPS Clerk 2021-22 final result will be announced?
A: IBPS Clerk 2021-22 final result have been announced on April 1.

Q: When IBPS Clerk 2021 mains exam will be conducted?
A: IBPS Clerk 2021 mains exam was conducted on January 25.

Q: Why IBPS Clerk 2021 recruitment process was put on hold by the Finance Ministry?
A: IBPS Clerk 2021 recruitment process was put on hold amid the language row. Concerns were raised regarding holding the exam in only two languages-English and Hindi despite the fact that there are 22 languages recognised by the constitution of India.

Q: How many banks participate in the recruitment process of IBPS Clerk?
A: A total of 11 public sector banks participate in the recruitment process of IBPS Clerk.

Q: What is the age limit to apply for IBPS Clerk exam?
A: The age limit to apply for IBPS Clerk exam is between 20 and 28 years.

Q: What is the basic pay of IBPS Clerk?
A: The initial basic pay of candidates selected through IBPS Clerk is INR 11,765.

Q: Is IBPS Clerk exam tough?
A: No, it is not tough to crack IBPS Clerk exam if candidates have prepared well.

Q: How many times can I give IBPS Clerk exam?
A: There is no limit to the number of attempts in IBPS Clerk exam. Candidates who fulfil the age limit prescribed by the examination authority can apply for the exam.

Q: Is graduation necessary to become IBPS Clerk?
A: Yes, graduation is necessary to apply for IBPS Clerk. The final year graduation candidates are not allowed to appear for the exam.

Q: What is the pass mark for IBPS Clerk?
A: The qualifying marks are prescribed by the examination authority for preliminary and mains exams of IBPS Clerk. Candidates are required to secure the minimum qualifying marks to qualify for both exams.

Q: Is computer knowledge necessary for IBPS Clerk?
A: Candidates must have an operating and working knowledge of the computer. Candidates must have a Certificate/Diploma/Degree in computer operations/Language/ should have studied Computer /Information Technology as one of the subjects in the High School/College/Institute.

Q: Do the marks of the IBPS Clerk preliminary exam count for the preparation of the final merit list?
A: No, the marks of the prelims exam are not counted for the final merit list. The final IBPS Clerk result is announced based on the performance of candidates in the mains exam.

Q: How is the IBPS Clerk cutoff calculated?
A: IBPS Clerk cut off is different for different categories. The cut off is calculated separately for preliminary and mains exams based on factors like difficulty level of exam, the number of test-takers, vacancies etc.

Q: What is the level of IBPS Clerk syllabus?
A: The level of IBPS Clerk syllabus is of high school and Graduation levels.
