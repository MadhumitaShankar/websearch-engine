What is CSIR NET?
Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) NET (also known as CSIR UGC NET) is a national-level exam conducted by the to determine the eligibility of candidates for Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and for Lectureship (LS)/ Assistant Professor in the universities and colleges of India. Candidates clearing the CSIR NET exam can become lecturers in the subject areas falling under the faculty of Science & Technology only.

CSIR NET 2021-22 Exam FAQs

Q: What is CSIR NET Exam for?
A: The CSIR NET exam is conducted to determine the eligibility of candidates for Junior Research Fellowships (JRF) or Lecturership (LS) in subjects related to the Science stream.

Q: When will CSIR NET result 2021 release?
A: The CSIR NET final result 2021 has been released by the CSIR on March 24, 2022, on the official website.

Q: When will CSIR NET cut off 2021 release?
A: The CSIR NET cut off 2021 has been released by the CSIR on March 25, 2022 subject-wise, category-wise and post-wise.

Q: What is the application fee for CSIR NET 2021 exam?
A: CSIR NET application fee is INR 1,000 for the General category, INR 500 for the OBC-NCL category and INR 250 for SC/ST category candidates. PWD candidates are exempted from the payment of the application fee.

Q: Is there any age criteria to apply for CSIR NET 2021 exam?
A: Yes, there is an age limit to apply for the CSIR NET 2021 exam. The age criterion is applicable only for candidates applying for JRF posts wherein candidates should be of a maximum of 28 years of age. There is no age limit for the Lecturer or Assistant Professor post.

Q: How many times is CSIR UGC NET conducted in a year?
A: CSIR UGC NET is conducted twice a year in typically the months of June and December. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the frequency of the conduct of the NET exam has been impacted.

Q: How many attempts are allowed for CSIR NET?
A: There is no limit on the number of attempts in the CSIR NET exam.

Q: In how many languages is the CSIR NET question paper available?
A: The question paper of the CSIR NET exam is available in two languages – Hindi and English.

Q: How much stipend is given to candidates qualifying the CSIR NET exam for JRF?
A: Qualifying candidates are given a stipend of INR 31,000 per month for the first two years. The stipend is increased to INR 35,000 for the third and subsequent years if the fellow is registered for a PhD.

Q: For which subjects is CSIR NET conducted?
A: CSIR NET is conducted for five Science subjects including Chemical Sciences, Earth, Atmospheric, Ocean and Planetary Sciences, Life Sciences, Mathematical Sciences, and Physical Sciences.

Q: How is CSIR NET different from UGC NET?
A: CSIR NET is conducted by NTA to shortlist aspirants for the award of JRF and to determine the eligibility of candidates for the Assistant Professor post for Science subjects only. On the other hand, the UGC NET is conducted by NTA to select candidates for the award of JRF and eligibility of Assistant Professor for all other subjects (total 81).

Q: How many papers are there in the CSIR NET exam?
A: There is only one paper in the CSIR NET exam. However, the question paper is divided into three parts- Part A, Part B and Part C.
