Commercial Law Eligibility & Entrance Exams
As mentioned above, candidates can pursue Commercial Law at both UG and PG levels. Given below is the basic Commercial Law eligibility criteria for both the levels:

UG Level
To pursue right after school, candidates should clear Class 12 in any stream (Science, Commerce, Humanities) from a recognised board with minimum 45-50 per cent aggregate.
integrated LLB course
Those interested can also pursue the after completing graduation in any subject from a recognised university.
LLB course

PG Level
Candidates can pursue if they possess an LLB degree with minimum 50-55 per cent aggregate from a recognised college/university.

Important FAQs regarding Commercial Law

Q: What does Commercial Law involve?


A: Commercial Law involves legal issues pertaining to business, trade, commerce and industry. The branch of Law resolves issues related to intellectual property, contract violation, consumer complaints, etc.

Q: What is the role of a Commercial Lawyer?
, etc.
A: The role of a Commercial Lawyer depends on the field in which s/he specialises. However, a typical day in the life of a Commercial Lawyer involves reading and revising contracts, drafting legal documents, reviewing mergers

Q: Who are the clients of a Commercial Lawyer?

A: Clients of a Commercial Lawyer usually include insurance companies, real estate companies, banks, government agencies, etc.

Q: What are the various Commercial Law branches?

A: Various branches of Commercial Law include Intellectual Property Law, Tax Law, Property Law, Corporate Law and so on.

Q: What are the qualities of a good Commercial Lawyer?

A: Some of the qualities that a good Commercial Lawyer should possess include analytical skills, commercial awareness, project management skills, negotiation skills, etc.

Q: How to enhance commercial awareness in Law?

A: To enhance commercial awareness, it is important to keep yourself up-to-date regarding the developments in the world of business such as mergers and acquisitions. You can read reputed newspapers such as Financial Times for the same.

Q: How to become a Commercial Lawyer?

A: To become a Commercial Lawyer, you first need to possess an LLB degree. You can enroll in an LLB course right after class 12 or even after graduation. Once the LLB programme is over, you can enroll in an LLM Commercial Law degree course. Those interested can also look for Commercial Law diploma courses at postgraduate level.

Q: What is the difference between Commercial Law and Business Law?
, Business Law, on the other hand, focuses on other aspects of business such as forming a company, mergers and acquisitions, shareholder rights, etc.
A: Commercial Law and Business Law are very similar in nature. While Commercial Law focuses on the sale and distribution of goods, as well as financing of certain transactions

Q: Which are the top entrance exams for Commercial Law?

A: Some of the top entrance exams that candidates can give for admission in LLM Commercial Law degree include CLAT PG, AILET PG and LSAT India PG.

Q: Which are the various job profiles in Commercial Law?

A: After completing a course in Commercial Law, candidates can look for jobs in profiles including Legal Advisor, Senior Associate, Legal Researcher, Legal Assistant and Contract Attorney.

Q: Is Corporate Law a part of Commercial Law?

A: Yes, Corporate Law is branch of Commercial Law. Candidates can pursue Corporate Law as a specialisation at UG/PG level.
