Ceramic and Glass designing course is a combination of creativity, science & technology. The Ceramic and Glass designing specialization is an exclusive and creative course for students who want to study designing as well as engineering for open-end career options in , Architecture and fields. Ceramic and Glass design course also explores , , research as well as efficient use and regulations for use of raw materials. The course deals with technology related to production of Glasses. The Glass and Ceramics based products, over the years have become luxurious and artistic with lightweight, energy-saving and wear-resistant options. A few examples are ceramic ceiling tiles, floor tiles, ceramic sanitary-wares, pottery, table-ware, ornamental-ware, biomedical implants, jet-engine turbines, bullet-resistant vests etc.
Interior Decoration
Engineering
material science
economics
Ceramic and Glass designing course is generally offered as a full-time degree like Bachelor of Design in Ceramic and Glass Design, Bachelor of Technology in Ceramic and Glass Design which are of 4 years duration.
As for the career scope, a degree in Ceramics and Glass Design provides various opportunities in Engineering, Housing/Real estate and Construction, Industrial Manufacturing and fields. The major segments for Glass Production are Container Glass, Specialty Glass, Flat Glass, and Fiberglass. As for Ceramic products, it is sanitary wares, refractories, cement, advanced ceramics and ceramic tiles. Some high-end products include Glass coated wires, UV Protected lenses, Gorilla Glass and premium glass packaging.

Ceramics and Glass Designer

A designer in ceramics work on ceramic sculpture, domestic and commercial tableware and kitchenware, garden ceramics, giftitems, jewellery, mirrors, glass art, wall and floor tiles.
They work at manufacturing units, client locations or as independent manufacturers.
They are familiar with technology that is used to design ceramics and glassware and, work in construction market to design high-end products

3 Lakhs and above


Ceramist

A ceramic designer or ceramist designs porcelain, stoneware and earthenware based potteries, tiles, bricks, jewellery etc.
Ceramist is more of an artist who works on her/his creativity for designing ceramics products including decorative painting, calligraphy etc.

3 Lakhs and above



Ceramics Engineer

An engineer with ceramic designing specialisation works on technology for manufacturing non-metallic and inorganic materials for Aerospace, Mining, Ceramic ware industry, construction industry, health care industry etc.

2.80 Lakhs and above


Glass Engineer and Technician

The designing of glass-based products involves core knowledge of its properties, processing and technology. They do research and development for glass processing such as tempering, cutting, edge grinding, modeling, molding as well as glass automation, design and application. A glass engineer works on latest technology for glass manufacturing to make it more durable, thin, light, and flawless as well as ecofriendly.

3 Lakhs and above

Important FAQs related to Ceramic & Glass Design

Q. What is Ceramic & Glass Designing course?
A. Ceramic & Glass Designing course is a mix of creativity, science and technology. The course deals with technology related to production of Glasses. The Glass and Ceramics based products, over the years have become luxurious and artistic with lightweight, energy-saving and wear-resistant options.

Q. What are the top entrance exams to get admission to Ceramic and Glass Design courses?
A. Popular entrance exams for getting admission to Ceramic and Glass Designing courses are NID Entrance Exam, Central Universities Common Entrance Test, JEE Main and JEE Advanced.

Q. What are the skills needed for Ceramic and Glass Design courses?
A. In order to pursue Ceramic and Glass Design courses candidates need to have the following skillset including Leadership skills, Creative skills, Research and Innovativeness skills, Knowledge of CAD technology, 3D model designing, Tools and techniques for hard material designing, Material Knowledge, Model making and manufacturing, Presentational Skills and many more.

Q. What are the popular job profiles after pursuing Ceramic and Glass Design courses?
A. Some of the popular job profiles after pursuing Ceramic and Glass Design courses are as follows

Ceramics Engineer
Ceramics and Glass Designer
Glass Engineer and Technician
Ceramist

Q. Which are the popular companies offering jobs to Ceramic and Glass Design graduates?
A. Popular companies offering jobs to Ceramic and Glass Design graduates are

Manthan

Q. What are the eligibility criteria for Ceramic and Glass Design courses?
A. In order to get admission to Ceramic and Glass Design courses candidate should have passed class 12th with any streams like Science/Commerce/Arts.

Q. What are the popular Ceramic and Glass Design courses?
A. Popular Ceramic and Glass Design courses are as follows

BDesign in Ceramic and Glass Design
BTech in Ceramic Engineering

Q. Which are the popular colleges for Ceramic & Glass Design course in India?
A. Two popular colleges offering Ceramic & Glass Design courses in India are as follows:

National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad
Kalapurnam Institute

Q. What is the role of a Ceramics Engineer?
A. The role of a Ceramics Engineer is to work on technology related to manufacturing non-metallic and inorganic materials used for Mining, Aerospace, Ceramic ware industry etc.

Q. What is the responsibility of a Glass Engineer and Technician?
A. The responsibility of a Glass Engineer and Technician is to do research and development for glass processing and he/she works on the latest technology for glass manufacturing.

Q. What is the role of a Ceramics and Glass Designer?
A. The Ceramics and Glass Designer works at manufacturing units and is responsible for designing ceramic sculpture, Jewellery, mirrors, glass art, wall and floor tiles etc.
