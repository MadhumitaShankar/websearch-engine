Bachelor in Medical Laboratory Technology is degree course which deals with detection, diagnosis and treatment of diseases with the help of clinical laboratory tests. MLT is also called Clinical Laboratory Science. It is and allied healthcare profession.
MLT students typically learn to perform clinical tests using various methods such as analysing body fluids, blood typing, chemical analysis and microorganism screening.

What are the career options after BMLT?
After the completion of BMLT, candidates can apply for jobs in a hospital, minor emergency centres, private laboratories, blood banks or clinics. A technician can become a technologist through further education and work experience. BMLT graduates can also pursue a master’s degree to open new career paths. They can choose to pursue MBA in Hospital Administration, to build a career in the administrative field. They can also prepare for the civil services exams post completing BMLT.

Eligibility for BMLT Degree
Candidates who wish to apply for the BMLT course can check the eligibility criteria for the course here

Applicant must have attained the age of 17 years as on December 31 of the year of admission
They must have passed Class 12 or equivalent from a recognised board/institute
They must have studied Physics, Chemistry and Biology as core subjects during Class 11 and 12

BMLT Entrance Exams
Most institutions in India admit candidates to BMLT courses in the basis of the marks obtained by them in Class 12. Candidates can directly apply for BMLT courses in these institutes by filling their application forms and depositing the fees online.
However, the top government institutions, such as AIIMS and PGIMER, admit candidates on the basis of their performance in an entrance test conducted by the respective institute. These entrance tests have Class 12 exit-level questions on Physics, Chemistry and Biology. Only those candidates who perform well in these exams are admitted to the respective institute.
A few popular entrance tests for BMLT are:


AIIMS Paramedical
JIPMER paramedical
PGIMER paramedical


BCECE Paramedical

BMLT: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
Q. What is the full form of BMLT?
A. BMLT stands for Bachelor in Medical Laboratory Technology. It is a 3.5 year paramedical degree course.
Q. Is BMLT from a private college worth it?
A. Yes, it is worth studying BMLT course from a private college. A candidate can make a decent living if they choose the right career path after completing BMLT.
Q. Can I do BMLT after Class 12?
A. Yes, you can apply for BMLT if you have passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry and Biology.
Q. Can we join BMLT without NEET?
A. Yes, NEET is not required for admission to BMLT courses. Most institutes in India admit candidates on the basis of the marks obtained in Class 12.
Q. What is the fees for BMLT course in India?
A. The fee for BMLT course ranges from Rs 250 per annum in AIIMS Delhi, to Rs 68,000 per semester in Lovely Professional University.
Q. What is the total duration of BMLT degree course?
A. To complete the BMLT degree course, you need to complete two stages; 3 years of academic studies and 6 months of internship. Hence, the total duration of BMLT degree course is 3.5 years.
