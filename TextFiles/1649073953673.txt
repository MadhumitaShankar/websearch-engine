Communication Engineering involves the study of systems, satellites, television, telephone, radar, and navigation etc. Communication Engineering is a new form of discipline. Various colleges in India offer Communications Engineering at the postgraduate level, thus leading to degrees. The role of a communication engineer is to research, develop, design, and be involved in the production of communication equipment.

Communications Engineering: Eligibility Criteria
If a student is planning to have a career in Communications Engineering degree then he/she should have to pursue an undergraduate engineering program (BTech) after completion of which students can opt for a job or for a postgraduation program in the same stream.

The students must have passed a BTech degree with a minimum passing percentage in the aggregation of all subjects studied in the undergraduate program.
The admissions are done through the entrance exam conducted for admission.

Communications Engineering: Types of Job Profiles and Recruiters
Communications Engineering offers opportunities in large numbers in the telecommunication sector, including, radio, television, telephone, and mobile communication, and computers, etc.
Communication engineers also find work opportunities with space agencies as technicians, engineers, and researchers. In academics, opportunities offered as lecturers, professors, and researchers.

-This sector includes companies, which have operations in legal, accounting, architectural, engineering, scientific and technical consulting services.

Technical services companies
-Communication engineers can seek employment in jobs at PSUs or work in DRDO, ISRO, BARC, and many others.

Government departments
-Communication engineers can work in central offices or electronic service centres.

Equipment manufacturers and installers of communication devices/systems
-Communication engineers can work with transport providers and help in forecasting the impact of new developments, write reports for planning authorities, and analyses and interpret the data gathered from transport studies.

Transport providers

Required Skillset for Communications Engineering
Communication skills, Technical knowledge, and Creativity are the fundamental skill sets to become a successful Communication Engineer. However, if you want to set yourself above in the crowd, here are some desirable qualities you must possess:




Good understanding of networks


Technical knowledge and ability


Adaptability and the ability to learn new skills


Organizational skills
Communication engineers can also work on managerial and technical roles. Those who gain experience in this field can choose to work as consultants on a contract basis. Their work will basically involve:

Managing/working with a team of engineers
Offering technical support
Undertaking surveys of sites
Testing systems
Designing/modifying designs
Writing reports





Important FAQs on Communications Engineering

Q. Is Communications Engineering hard?
A. This course is very tough. Candidates need to pass the entrance examination to get admission in any of the institutes. Most of the students shift to other courses because of the difficulty level of the course curriculum.

Q. Is Communications Engineering better than Computer Science Engineering?
A. No, Communications Engineering is quite tough compared to computer science engineering.

Q. Who is the pioneer of electronics and Communications Engineering?
A. Claude Elwood Shannon is considered the pioneer of Electronics and Communications Engineering.

Q. Is it necessary for communication engineers to know how to code?
A. Yes, communication engineers must have a good knowledge of C programming which can help them get a job in the IT industry and core industry.

Q. What do the Communication engineers will do?
A. Communications engineers are responsible for the research, design, development, and production of communications equipment and systems. Some of the duties include designing, developing, installing, and testing system equipment and implementing procedures to make all components operational.
