What is CEED?
Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay () conducts Common Entrance Examination for Design (CEED) to provide admission to aspirants in and programmes offered at colleges accepting CEED scores. Each year, more than 5,000 students appear for the CEED exam.
IIT Bombay
Master of Design (MDes)
PhD



CEED exam has two parts – A and B. Part A consists of objective type questions (to be attempted in online mode) whereas part B of the exam contains subjective questions (to be attempted in offline mode). CEED Part B tests the design, drawing and writing skills of the candidate.

CEED Result 2022
The results of CEED 2022 has been declared by IIT Bombay on March 8. The results would include the marks obtained by candidates in Part A for all candidates who have appeared in the CEED 2022 entrance exam. FAQs on CEED 2022

Q. When will the CEED 2022 cutoff marks for Part A be released?
A. The cutoff marks for Part A has been released on February 10. Those who meet the cutoff in their respective categories will be shortlisted and their Part B answer books will be evaluated.

Q. When will the CEED 2022 scorecard be released?
A: The scorecard for CEED 2022 is available from March 12. The last date to download the scorecard is June 14, 2022.

Q:
When will CEED 2022 results be declared?

A: CEED 2022 results have been declared on March 8. Results have been declared for all candidates who have appeared in the CEED 2022 entrance exam.

Q: Is it mandatory to clear both Part A and B of CEED exam?
A: Yes, it is mandatory to clear both parts of CEED exam. Part A of the exam is considered to be a qualifying test. Thus, candidates who do not clear part A are not evaluated for part B of the exam.

Q: How many times is CEED exam conducted in a year?
A: CEED is conducted once a year, usually, in the month of January. The result for CEED written exam is released in March. In 2022, the exam will be held on January 23 and result will be declared on March 10.

Q. Who can apply for CEED exam?
A. Candidates meet CEED eligibility criteria if they have secured 55% marks (50% or reserved category students) in their qualifying exam. As per authorities, candidates meet the eligibility criteria if they:

Have completed a degree/ diploma/ postgraduate degree programme of minimum three years (after 10+2 level)
Are appearing for the final examination of their degree/ diploma/ postgraduate degree programme by July 2020
Have passed the GD Arts diploma programme (10+5 level) by July 2020

Q. What is the application fee for CEED 2022?
Candidates can pay the application fee for CEED exam online only. CEED 2022 application fee for women/ SC/ ST/ PwD candidates is Rs 1,600 and for all other candidates, the application fee is Rs 3,200.

A. Is CEED exam tough?
The difficulty level of CEED exam has been maintained by authorities over the years. However, most design aspirants share that if difficulty level of all design entrance exams conducted for PG level courses is compared then CEED is slightly easier than . This is primarily because the drawing questions asked in NID DAT are considered to be tougher than those put forth in CEED exam.

A. Seats available for CEED admission vary from one institute to the other (that accept the exam scores for admission). For eg, IIT Delhi has 20 seats on offer for students who clear CEED exam. Candidates are informed that CEED is just a qualifying exam. Thus, any aspirant who clears CEED exam needs to further appear for admission rounds as conducted by the college where they want to apply for admission.
