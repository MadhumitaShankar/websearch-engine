Dairy Technology is a science and engineering field that deals with the study of milk processing and its products. It is a part of food technology and processing industry that involves processing, packaging, distribution and transportation of various dairy products such as milk and ice-cream by using the science of biochemistry, bacteriology, and nutrition. The field of dairy technology basically uses “technology” to make dairy products hi-tech as well as useful.
There are different dairy technology courses available for candidates at undergraduate and postgraduate level. Candidates can choose to pursue a degree or diploma course in dairy technology. Some of the popular dairy technology courses include BTech, BSc, MTech, and MSc.

Dairy Technology: Course curriculum
Subjects taught in dairy technology depend on the course (BSc/ BTech/ MSc/ MTech/ diploma) as well as the specialisation a candidate is pursuing. There are various specialisations available in dairy technology including dairy chemistry, dairy engineering, dairy economics, dairy cattle nutrition, dairy cattle physiology, etc.
Given below is the course curriculum for two popular dairy technology courses: BTech dairy technology and MTech dairy technology.
BTech dairy technology is a four-year duration course typically spread over eight semesters. Subjects that are usually taught in BTech dairy technology include:





BTech Dairy Technology Syllabus


Physical Chemistry of Milk

Milk Production Management & Dairy Development


Engineering Drawing

Dairy Technology: Job Profiles & Top Recruiters
The field of dairy technology plays an important role in India’s agriculture-based industry. The rapid expansion of dairy industry in India has created huge demand for skilled professionals in this field. Therefore, candidates pursuing a course in dairy technology have a lot of scope and career opportunities in this field.
A graduate in dairy technology can find jobs in both public and private sectors at places such as rural banks, manufacturing firms, milk product processing and dairy farms, etc. Given below are the job profiles available for dairy technology graduates and postgraduates:

: A dairy technologist is responsible for developing new and more effective methods of production, preservation, and utilization of dairy products using the principles of physics, chemistry, economics, engineering, and bacteriology.
Dairy Technologist
: A microbiologist monitors and studies microbes such as bacteria, fungi and viruses by using a variety of analytical and scientific techniques. A microbiologist usually studies organisms that cause disease and environmental damage or are of agricultural or industrial interest.
Microbiologist
: A dairy nutritionist is directly involved with the dietary management of dairy cattle herds. The main aim of a dairy nutritionist is to ensure maximum production while maintaining overall herd health.
Dairy Nutritionist
: A dairy scientist is an agricultural scientist who helps increasing milk production and streamlining milk processing. The scientist also helps in improving animal welfare and productivity through more efficient means of producing as well as using milk.
Dairy Scientist
: A dairy production manager is responsible for assisting the manager in the financial and physical performance of the farm. The manager implements short and long term business plans and ensures optimizing the performance of the farm.
Dairy Production Manager
: A dairy manager is mostly involved with the supervision of workers in all aspects of dairy production such as preparing the required feed, maintaining the milking equipment, managing the herd, and so on.
Dairy Manager
: The responsibility of a logistics and distribution manager is to organise the storage and distribution of goods. A logistic ensures that right products are delivered to the right location at the right time.
Logistics and Distribution Manager
: A farm manager is responsible for directing and coordinating worker activities such as irrigation, harvesting, grading, payroll, recordkeeping, etc. A farm manager sometimes also contracts with farmers for raising of crops or management of crop production.
Farm Manager
: A quality control officer’s responsibility is to ensure that dairy products meet the required quality and efficiency standards.
Quality Control Officer

Dairy Technology: FAQs

Q. What is Dairy Technology?

A. It is a part of food technology and processing industry that deals with engineering to process milk & milk products.

Q. What is the eligibility criteria to apply for admission to Dairy Technology undergraduate course?

A. Candidates must have completed Class 12 with at least 50% to 60% marks.

Q. What is the eligibility criteria to apply for admission to Dairy Technology postgraduate course?

A. Candidates must possess degree in Dairy Technology with at least 50% marks from a recognized university/institute.

Q. What is the duration of Dairy Technology undergraduate course?

A. It is a four-year duration course.

Q. What is the duration of Dairy Technology postgraduate course?

A. It is a two-year duration course.

Q. What is the career lookout for a Dairy Technology degree holder?

A. An individual holding Dairy Technology degree can apply for job profiles like Dairy Technologist, Microbiologist, Dairy Nutritionist, Dairy Production Manager, Quality Control Officer, etc.

Q. Which are the best companies to work for a Dairy Technology degree holder?

A. There are many renowned companies like Amul, Nestle, HCL, Britannia Industries, Cadbury, Mother Dairy, etc.

Q. Is it a correct decision to make career in Dairy Technology domain?

A. Yes, it can be a good decision as the food industry is touching new heights. But candidates must possess the skill-set required for job like team work, problem solving skills, scientific knowledge, etc.
