Telecommunications Engineering is a four-year study in engineering discipline centered around electrical and computer engineering. The field of engineering work ranges from basic circuit design to strategic mass developments. Telecommunication engineers design, test and build the various technologies that allow people to communicate over distances. They work in fields like telephone and internet networks, broadcasting and satellite communications, and radio and television. After studying Telecommunication engineering, students have a career as electronics engineers, electronics and communication engineer, electronics design & development engineers, desktop support engineers, system control engineers.

Telecommunication Engineering: Eligibility Criteria
The eligibility criteria for BE Telecommunication Engineering is given below:

Candidates must have passed the class 12 exam or equivalent and secured a minimum of 50% aggregate marks.
Candidates must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as core subjects with Biology/ Computer Science and Electronics as optional subjects
Students belonging to the reserved category are provided with a certain relaxation in minimum aggregate scores.
The eligibility criteria for MTech in Telecommunication Engineering is as follows

Students must have a graduation degree in the field of Telecommunication Engineering or any other related field.
The marks obtained during graduation should not be lower than a total aggregate of 60%.
The admission to MTech in Telecommunication is based on marks obtained in the entrance exam combined with performance in personal interview/group discussion based on the college/university.

FAQs related to Telecommunication Engineering

Q. Is Telecommunication Engineering a good career?
A. Yes, the Telecommunication Engineering offers a wide range of career options in the field of network and communication, radio, television, quality and software systems.

Q. Is Telecommunication Engineering in demand?
A. Since the telecom job industry keeps growing, more professionals are in high demand. Jobs ranging from telecom engineers, installers, data analysts and so on are in need of more hands.

Q. Is telecommunication engineering hard?
A. Telecommunications requires lots of mathematics, calculus, circuit theories, complex analysis, electromagnetic waves, electronic circuits, numerical reasoning and more and more calculations.

Q. How can I get a job in the telecom industry?
A. To become a telecom and network system administrator, you will need at least a bachelor's degree and complete courses in networking and Telecom technologies, Protocol testing, and Network designing & Optimization and so on.

Q. What degree is needed for telecommunications?
A. Although there is no specific degree required to work as a telecommunications specialist, some employers require an associate's degree. Potential candidates may also need a degree in the field, while some employers will accept candidates who only have extensive experience

Q. What can a telecommunication engineer do?
A. Telecommunications engineers design, test and build the various technologies that allow people to communicate over distances.

Q. What are the subjects in telecommunication engineering?
A. Some of the course subjects are Waveguide Components, Neural networks, Network Analysis, Digital Communication, Digital Signal Processing, Engineering Physics, Information Coding, Analog Communication.
