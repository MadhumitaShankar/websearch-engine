IBPS RRB 2021 final allotment list has been released on January 31. The lists have been released separately for Officers Scale I, Office Assistant, Officers Scale II and Officers Scale III. The lists display the registration number, marks obtained and allotted bank. IBPS RRB 2021 final allotment result was announced on January 1. The last date to check the result was January 31.
IBPS RRB 2022 exam dates have been announced. The prelims exam of IBPS RRB for Officers Scale I and Office Assistant (Multipurpose) will be conducted on August 7, 13, 14, 20 and 21. The single online exam for Officers Scale II and III will be conducted on September 24. The mains exam for Officers Scale I and Office Assistant (Multipurpose) will be conducted on September 24 and October 1, respectively.

What is IBPS RRB Recruitment?
IBPS RRB is a national-level banking exam conducted every year by the for candidates who aspire to join the Regional Rural Bank (RRBs). Recruitment is carried out for the following
Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS)
Group A and B posts.



Post Name
Post Name

Office Assistant
Marketing Manager

Treasury Manager
Officers Scale I

Banking Officer Scale-II
Agriculture Officer (Grade-II)

Law Officer (Grade-II)
Chartered Accountant (Grade-II)

IT Officer (Grade-II)
Officer (Grade III)
For Officer level posts, candidates are selected based on preliminary exam, mains/single level exam, and interview. For Office Assistant (Multipurpose)/Clerk post, candidates are selected based on preliminary and mains exams. Candidates provisionally shortlisted are allotted to one of the participating RRBs based on the number of vacancies and merit-cum-preference.
Aspirants must also read about . The organisation has been formed by the Union Cabinet to conduct a Common Eligibility Test (CET) to appoint candidates in Non-Technical and Non-Gazetted Group B and C posts under , , and IBPS. NRA CET will serve as a screening/preliminary exam for subsequent stages of the selection process. NRA will only conduct the Prelims/Tier-I exam, while the other stages of selection will be conducted by the respective examination bodies.

The post-wise roles and responsibilities of candidates selected under IBPS RRB recruitment are given below:

IBPS RRB Exam Day Guidelines
Candidates can check below IBPS RRB exam day guidelines:

Reporting Time

The reporting time is different for different candidates
Candidates are required to report much before the reporting time to avoid crowding
Candidates are required to report at least 15 minutes before the reporting time indicated on the admit card
Candidates' roll numbers and lab numbers are not displayed outside the exam venue. They are informed individually at the entry gate, post admit card and ID verification

Items permitted inside the exam centre

Candidates are required to wear a mask
Candidates can carry a personal transparent water bottle and hand sanitiser of 50 ml
Candidates are allowed to carry pen
Candidates are required to carry exam-related documents such as admit card, ID card in the original, photocopy of ID Card, etc
Candidates are required to submit a health self-declaration
The temperature of candidates is checked at the entry gate through thermo guns

Candidate registration

Candidates' registrations are done through photo capture
Photographs are taken while candidates are standing
The seat number is given to the candidate after registration

Rough sheet, call letter and ID proof management

Rough sheets are kept at each candidate's desk. No additional rough sheets are provided
The call letter and ID proof copy are required to be dropped in the boxes provided at the exit of lab/venue
Candidates are required to drop the rough sheets in the boxes provided at the exit of the lab/venue while leaving the exam centres
The drop boxes are different for the rough sheets and call letters

Banned items

Any stationery item such as textual material (printed or written), bits of papers, geometry/pencil box, plastic pouch, calculator, scale, writing pad, pen drives, log table, electronic pen/scanner, etc are not allowed inside the exam centre
Any communication device such as mobile phone, Bluetooth, earphones, microphones, pager, health band, etc. are not allowed
Other items such as sunglasses, handbags, hair-pin, hair-band, belt, cap, etc. are also not allowed
All ornament such as ring, earrings, nose-pin, chain/necklace, pendants, badge, brooch, etc. are thoroughly checked
Watch, camera, or any metallic item are not allowed
Any eatable item opened or packed, water bottle, etc. are not allowed
Any other item which could be used for unfair means for hiding communication devices such as camera, bluetooth devices, etc. are not allowed

Iris Scan
Iris is scanned at the exam venue in lieu of a biometric thumb impression due to the COVID-19 situation. The process is given below:

Iris of the left eye is captured for all the candidates
Candidates are required to remove contact lenses and spectacles while capturing Iris
Half feet distance between scanner and eye is maintained

IBPS RRB FAQs 2021

Q. When IBPS RRB 2021 exam will be conducted?
A. The prelims exam of IBPS RRB for Officers Scale I and Office Assistant (Multipurpose) posts was conducted on August 1, 7, 8, and 14. The mains exam and single online level for Officers Scale I, II and III was conducted on September 25, 2021. The mains exam for Office Assistant was conducted on October 17, 2021. The interview was conducted from November 8, 2021 onwards.

Q. When IBPS RRB 2021 notification was released?
A. IBPS RRB 2021 notification was released on June 7 by IBPS. The notification was made available in PDF form and comprised details related to the exam.

Q. When IBPS RRB application form 2021 was released?
A. The application process of IBPS RRB had started on June 8. The window to fill the application form closed on June 28.

Q. How do I prepare for IBPS RRB exam?
A. For prelims exam, you need to focus on Reasoning and Maths. Make a list of topics in Reasoning such as blood relation, seating arrangement, direction puzzle, etc. and study each topic and then do a lot of practice. For Maths, practice shortcuts, clear basic concepts, practice regularly and learn theorems. For mains, you need to focus on Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness, English/Hindi Language, and Computer Knowledge. For General Awareness, focus on the latest events of the last six months. For English, improve your grammar and vocabulary by reading books and through practice. For the Computer section, learn the basics, networking, hardware, software, internet, windows, excel, etc.

Q. Which is easy to crack IBPS RRB PO or IBPS PO?
A. IBPS RRB PO is comparatively easier than the . The level of questions asked in IBPS RRB PO exam is not that high. The growth in IBPS PO exam is more than IBPS RRB exam.
IBPS PO exam

Q. What is the career growth of IBPS RRB PO?
A. Once candidates get through the recruitment process of IBPS RRB, they start working as Assistant Managers. Candidates get a lot of opportunities to grow in Regional Rural Banks. They will get promoted to Manager after completion of two years' probation period. Banks conduct exams at a regular interval of time for the promotion of the employees.

Q. What type of questions are asked in IBPS RRB PO Interview?
A. For interview, candidates must prepare current affairs, banking awareness questions, agriculture crops, priority sector loans, family and educational background, etc.

Q. Can I get my home state as a location after passing the bank IBPS RRB exam?
A. Most of the time, one gets posted in their respective home states. While filling the application form, you must choose the banks depending on your language/state.

Q. Is IBPS RRB exam conducted every year?
A. Yes, IBPS RRB exam is conducted every year by IBPS to select eligible candidates for the post of Officers Scale I, II, III, and Office Assistant in participating Regional Rural Banks.

Q. How many candidates apply for IBPS RRB exam?
A. In 2020-21, total 7.06 lakh candidates had registered for Office Assistant while 5.95 lakh candidates had registered for Officers.

Q. Is a resume necessary for the IBPS RRB PO interview?
A. Resume or CV is not necessary for IBPS RRB PO interview. Candidates will get a bio-data form, which they need to fill while appearing for the interview. Candidates need to carry the necessary documents prescribed by the exam authority.
