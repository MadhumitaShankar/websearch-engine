SSC CGL 2022 application status has been released by all the regions except the Northern, North-Western Region. The candidates can check their application status to know their SSC CGL exam date and venue of examination. SSC CGL admit cards will be released five days before the exam date. The admit card will be available to download till the exam date of the candidate. This is done to reduce the burden on the regional websites of SSC providing the admit cards for their region. The exam will be conducted in multiple shifts and the final score will arrive after the normalisation of marks. These marks will be calculated based on the final answer key.
SSC CGL Tier-I exam 2022 will be conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT) from April 11 to 21.
The Commission will release the tentative answer key and candidates' responses within ten days of the completion of the exam. SSC will invite objections from the candidates regarding the incorrect questions/answers. After considering the objections, the Commission will prepare a final answer key based on which the result will be released.

What is SSC CGL?
SSC CGL is a national-level exam conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) to shortlist candidates for Group-B and -C posts in various ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India. Every year, about 30 lakh candidates fill out the SSC CGL application form and about 15 lakh aspirants appear for the exam. The Staff Selection Commission conducts the exam in four stages called tiers. The first two tiers of the Combined Graduate Level (CGL) exam are conducted in online mode. The next two tiers of the exam are held in offline mode.

How to apply for SSC CGL 2022 Exam?
Candidates would have to fill SSC CGL 2022 application form on the official website of the Commission in two parts - registration and application. The steps to fill out the application form are given below.
Step 1: Visit the official website of SSC - ssc.nic.in.
Step 2: Click on the 'New User? Register Now 'link.
Step 3: Fill the SSC CGL registration form.
Step 4: Log in to the registered account using the registration number (generated at the time of registration) and password (set at the time of registration).
Step 5: Fill in all details asked in the SSC CGL application form.
Step 6: Upload scanned images of photos and signatures and other important documents, if applicable.
Step 7: Review the SSC online form carefully and click on the Final Submit button.
Step 8: Pay the application fee of INR 100 in online or offline mode.

SSC CGL Selection Process 2022
SSC CGL selection process will include the following stages/tiers:

Candidates need to fill out the application form online on the official website of the commission in a specified manner. The application fee is INR 100.
Application process:
: SSC regional websites release the admit for the Tier-I exam. The candidates need to download their admit cards from these regional SSC websites in a specified manner.
Tier-I admit cards
: Tier-I is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) that contains objective-type Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) of 200 marks.
Tier-I exam
: The result is declared by SSC on its official website within 45 days of the exam. The result contains the names and/or roll numbers of candidates qualifying for the Tier-II exam.
Tier-I result
: Eligible candidates need to download their admit cards for the Tier-II exam from the official regional websites. The admit card is typically released about one week before the exam.
Tier-II admit card
: Tier-II is again a CBT that is divided into four papers.
Tier-II exam
: The result for tier-II is declared on the official website of the commission in the form of a merit list. Candidates clearing the tier-II exam are called to appear for the Tier-III paper.
Tier-II result
: The Tier-III exam is conducted after the Tier-III exam and the gap between the exams is from one week to ten days only. So the Tier-II admit cards are released after the Tier-II admit cards.
Tier-III admits card
: Tier-III is a pen-and-paper based exam that is descriptive in nature.
Tier-III exam
: Similar to tier-I and -II results, candidates can check CGL results for tier-III also in online mode only. Candidates clearing the tier-III exam need to appear for the final stage – Tier-IV.
Tier-III result
: Like admit cards for other tiers, Tier-IV admit cards are also available for download on the regional websites of SSC
Tier-IV admit card
: In tier-IV, SSC conducts either a computer proficiency test, skill test or document verification, as per the post applied by the candidate. SSC CGL tier-IV is only a qualifying paper. It means that marks scored by candidates in tier-IV are not calculated at the time of declaration of the final result.
Tier-IV exam
: Final result is announced based on candidates' performance in tier-I, tier-II and tier-III exams. All qualifying candidates are then allotted Group-B and C posts in various ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India.

SSC CGL 2022 FAQs

Q: When will SSC CGL 2022 Application Status be released?
A: SSC CGL 2022 application status has been released by all the regions except Northern and North-Western regions. Get here details and direct link to check application status.

Q: When will SSC CGL 2022 exam be conducted?
A: SSC CGL 2022 tier-I exam will be conducted from April 11 to 21 as CBT in multiple shifts at the designated exam centres across the nation.

Q: Will SSC CGL be conducted in 2022?
A: Yes, SSC CGL 2022 exam (Tier-I) will be conducted from April 11 to 21, 2022.

Q: Who can apply for SSC CGL 2022?
A: The eligibility conditions for SSC CGL 2022 exam were mentioned in the official notification. The candidates who are graduates in any stream and fulfil the age limit could apply for the CGL exam. See
SSC CGL eligibility conditions in detail

Q: Can I apply now for SSC CGL 2022 exam?
A: No, the SSC CGL application 2022 was closed on January 23, 2022.

Q: Is SSC CGL exam held in online mode?
A: The first two tiers of CGL exam are held in online mode and the other two tiers of the exam are conducted in offline mode.

Q: Is it mandatory to appear for all the tiers of SSC CGL exam?
A: Yes, candidates need to appear for all the tiers of SSC CGL in order to qualify the exam.

Q: How many stages does SSC CGL exam contain?
A: SSC CGL exam contains four stages - Tier-I (online exam), Tier-II (online exam), Tier-III (offline exam) and Tier-IV (computer proficiency test/skill test/document verification).

Q: Is there any negative marking in SSC CGL exam?
A: Yes, there is a negative marking of 0.50 marks in the tier-I exam.

Q: What is the minimum eligibility criteria to apply for SSC CGL exam?
A: Candidates who have completed graduation from a recognised university are eligible for the SSC CGL exam.

Q: How many SSC CGL vacancies are released every year?
A: SSC CGL 2022 vacancies were not released in the official notification. It will be released later. The SSC CGL exam is conducted for a good number of vacancies every year.

Q: For which posts is SSC CGL exam conducted?
A: SSC CGL exam is conducted for various Group-B (Gazetted), Group-B (Non-Gazetted) and Group -C posts available in different ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India.

Q: How many times is SSC CGL conducted in a year?
A: Each stage (tier-I, II, III and IV) of SSC CGL exam is conducted once a year.

Q: Was there an age limit to appear for SSC CGL exam?
A: SSC CGL age limit differs as per the post applied. However, candidates should be minimum 18 years in age to apply for any SSC CGL post.
