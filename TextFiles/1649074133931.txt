SSC Junior Hindi Translator 2021-22 exam will be conducted in December 2022. The notification for the exam will be released on August 22, 2022. The last date to apply for the exam is September 21, 2022.
Staff Selection Commission (SSC) had announced SSC JHT 2020 final result and cutoff marks on October 29. On the basis of the final result, a total of 182 candidates have been selected for appointment. SSC JHT 2020-21 document verification was conducted on August 30 and 31, 2021.

What is SSC JHT?
Staff Selection Commission conducts an open competitive examination for recruitment of Junior Hindi Translator, Senior Hindi Translator for various Ministries/Departments/Organizations in the Government of India. The recruitment is done for the following posts.



Code
Name of post
Salary (In INR)

A
Junior Translator in Central Secretariat Official Language Service (CSOLS)
Level-6 (35,400- 1,12,400)

B
Junior Translator in M/o Railways (Railway Board)
Level-6 (35,400-1,12,400)

C
Junior Translator in Armed Forces Headquarters (AFHQ)
Level-6 (35,400-1,12,400)

D
Junior Translator (JT)/Junior Hindi Translator (JHT) in subordinate offices who have adopted Model RRs of DoP&T for JT/ JHT
Level-6 (35,400-1,12,400)

E
Senior Hindi Translator in various Central Government Ministries/Departments/Offices
Level-7 (44,900-1,42,400)

SSC JHT 2020-21 FAQs

Q. When is SSC JHT JHT 2020 final result will be announced?
A. SSC JHT 2020 final result was announced on October 29.

Q. Is the exam conducted every year?
A. Yes, the exam is conducted every year depending on the number of vacancies reported by the concerned departments.

Q. In which mode the exam is conducted?
A. The exam is conducted in both online and offline mode. The objective type paper is conducted online while the descriptive paper is held offline.

Q. How many candidates appear in the exam?
A. Approximately, 12,000 candidates appear in the exam for recruitment to the post of Junior Hindi Translator, Senior Hindi Translator for various Ministries/Departments/Organizations in the Government of India.

Q. How many papers are there in the exam?
A. The exam comprises two papers:1 and 2. Paper 1 is an objective-type paper while Paper 2 is a descriptive-type paper.

Q. What are the type of questions asked in SSC JHT exam?
A. SSC JHT exam comprises both objective and conventional type questions.

Q. How are candidates selected for the exam?
A. Candidates are selected based on their performance in both Paper 1 and 2. Candidates qualifying both the papers are called for document verification before final allocation.

Q. How to apply for SSC JHT exam?
A. Candidates need to fill SSC JHT application form online only. No other mode of application is accepted for the recruitment exam.

Q. What is the exam fee?
A. The application fee is INR 100 while SC/Women/ST/ESM candidates are exempted from fee payment.

Q. How can I pay the application fee?
A. The application fee can be paid both online and offline.

Q. Is there any normalisation of marks?
A. Yes, there is normalisation of marks because the exam is held in multiple sessions. The normalisation of marks is done to adjust the difficulty level of question papers held across various sessions.

Q. Does SSC release the answer key?
A. Yes, SSC releases the JHT answer key. First, the provisional answer key is made available following which the final answer key is released.
