Admission Process


NEET
The Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) is an undergraduate degree programme in the medical field. A person holding the MBBS degree becomes a certified medical practitioner. It takes 5.5 years for the completion of the degree including one year of rotational internship at hospitals, health centres, and health camps organised by non-profit organisations. MBBS course includes the study of human anatomy, human cytology, medicine, chemistry, pharmaceutical, chemistry, drugs formulation & effect and method of surgery. After completing the MBBS degree, one can start practising in their own clinic or get a job in any hospital. The candidates must have completed their class 12th exams with Physics, Chemistry and Biology from a recognised institute.
Medical colleges in Bangalore accepts the National Eligibility Entrance Test (NEET) entrance examination score.
Medical Colleges in Bangalore Eligibility Criteria
The following are the eligibility criteria for admission to an MBBS course in India:

Candidates must have passed Class 12 with Science subjects i.e. Physics, Chemistry and Biology. They also must have studied English as the main subject for their qualifying examination
Moreover, candidates need to fulfil the age limit criteria i.e. they must have completed 17 years of age at the time of admissions
They must qualify NEET entrance exam
Medical Colleges in Bangalore with NIRF Ranking and Shiksha Rating (Aggregate)
NIRF ranking is provided every year by the Ministry of Education based on the parameters Teaching, Learning & Resources (TLR), Research and Professional Practice (RP), Graduation Outcomes (GO), Outreach and Inclusivity (OI) and Peer Perception. Shiksha ratings are given by the students on the basis of campus life, faculty, infrastructure and placement of the college. None of the Medical Colleges in Bangalore made it to the NIRF ranking 2021.

ROI of Top Medical Colleges in Bangalore
Return on Investment or ROI shows how beneficial it is to pursue a particular course from a particular college. It is the comparison between the fee invested by the student while studying and the return he/she gets in the form of placement. In the case of popular engineering courses, selecting a college or university based on the ROI factor can be very much valued. This is helpful when it becomes difficult to choose between two shortlisted colleges.

Q: Is it better to do an MBBS from a private or government college?
A: Studying MBBS in private or government college does not make any difference to candidates' medical careers. However, the tuition fees of private medical colleges can be 10 times higher than that of government colleges. Also, if one manages to secure a seat in a premier government medical institute like AIIMS Delhi, the candidate will get exposure to an intense focus on academics and learning and also a host of extra-curricular activities. The campus provides world-class facilities for sports as well as recreation.

Q: What is the fees and duration of the MBBS programme?
A: The MBBS fees for the government medical colleges in India range between Rs 10,000 - Rs 50,000 and for the private colleges, it can be in the range of Rs 2,00,000 to Rs 22,00,000. The total duration of the MBBS course is 5.5 years including 4.5 years of academics and 1 year of internship.

Q: What does MBBS doctor do?
A: The MBBS graduates work as physicians and prescribes medicines and treatments for various diseases. They get hired by hospitals, nursing homes, medical colleges, health corporations, medical health society, health ministry, pharmaceutical industries and back office medical consultancy.

Q: What is the difference between MBBS and MD?
A: The MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) is an undergraduate course in medical and MD (Doctor of Medicine) is a postgraduate course that the doctors can pursue after completing their MBBS degree.

Q: What is the average salary package of the MBBS graduate?
A: The average annual salary package offered to the general physician in India is Rs 4-5 lakh per annum.

Q: Which are the top recruiters of MBBS graduates in India?
A: The top recruiters of MBBS graduates in India are Medanta Hospitals, Fortis Healthcare Ltd, Sun Pharmaceutical Industries Ltd, Apollo Munich Health, Industries Co Ltd, Sri Ganga Ram Hospital, Lilavati Hospital and Research Centre, Wockhardt Ltd, Apollo Hospitals Enterprises Ltd, Cipla Ltd, Religare Health Insurance Company Ltd, Indraprastha Apollo Hospitals, All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Postgraduate Institute of Medical Education and Research.

Q: What is the eligibility required for pursuing MBBS in India?
A: The candidate must have completed their 12 class examination with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English and must have secured a minimum aggregate of 50% in the core subjects (PCB). Then, to take admission into MBBS programmes offered by medical institutes in India, the candidates need to take the NEET entrance exams.
