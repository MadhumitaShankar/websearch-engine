Clinical Research refers to a study conducted to assess potential treatment and its effect on human beings. Clinical trials help us find out if medication, a device, a diagnostic product or a treatment regimen is safe and effective for use. During a trial, increasing information is gained about the treatment option, its risks and how it may work. Certification in clinical research (CCR) is meant to offer candidates a chance to obtain and develop the skill essential for effective clinical research. The objective of various programmes in clinical research is to meet the high demand for qualified personnel for human clinical trials. Clinical research courses enable you to know the practical features of clinical trial and its management including designing the phases, implementation, planning, regulatory and procedural guidelines, data analysis, and ethical considerations.

Diploma in Clinical Research
This programme is designed in such a way that it assists the aspiring candidates in learning about the bioethics that goes in a hospital or a clinic. The course curriculum is such that it provides the student with the right skills needed to assist in the preparation of presentations and manuscripts of scientific meetings and technical medicine journals.
The programme is built to produce quality clinical research analysts having the right skill set required to work in the clinics and hospitals. Diploma in Clinical Research course opens a vast range of career options for the candidates. From working in the medicine department such as clinics and hospitals to working as a teacher or lecturer at a college, candidates can select to pursue anything they wish to.
Diploma in Clinical Research is a 1-year course. The minimum eligibility for admission is a 55 per cent score in graduation in the Medical science courses (BDS, MBBS, etc.) or a Degree in Pharmacology, Science, Pharmacy, Biotechnology, Life Sciences, etc., pursued from a well-known university. In most of the institutes, the course is yearly in nature with term-end examinations conducted at the end of the year by individual universities.
For the universities conducting their entrance test, admission to the course is based on a candidate’s performance in the particular entrance test in addition to the merit in graduation degree. Certain universities do not conduct an entrance examination and admit candidates based on their overall merit in the last graduation or higher education degree.

Clinical Research: Career Prospects and Job Opportunities
As per the latest studies the Clinical Research field which is at present having business growth around 250 crores will soon have a growth of approximately 5000 crores. This profession assists investigators to end up with good income similar to doctors. A lot of research sectors such as Biotech Companies, Pharma Industries, Research Labs, etc have opened job opportunities for clinical investigators. Clinical Research is an outstanding career option for the candidates, keen to learn about the know-how of research in the medicinal and clinical field. Such individuals get to work as Clinical Research Physicians, Clinical Research Associates, Biostatistician, Clinical Research Coordinator, Clinical Research Analyst, etc.

Clinical Research: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q. What is Clinical Research?
A. Clinical Research is branch of medical science which deals with research part to assess the scope of the potential treatment methods for human being including medication, a device, a diagnostic product.

Q. What are the courses available in Clinical Research?
A. Candidates can do various courses such as certificate, diploma, and undergraduate or postgraduate degree courses in Clinical Research. Candidates are advised to check the eligiblity of each course before applying for the exam.

Q. What is the eligibility criteria for admission to Postgraduate course in Clinical Research?
A. Candidates should have undergraduate degree in clinical research for seeking admission to master course in this field.

Q. What are the top exams for admission to clinical research courses?
A. Colleges provide admission on the basis of merit or scores in entrance test. Some colleges provides admission in clinical research courses based on the total scores of class 12th and the entrance test. Some of the entrance exams are NEET, ICRI entrance test, and NIMS.

Q. What is the career scope in the field of Clinical Research?
A. As medical field is growing like never before, clinical research provides bright career scope to candidates. After finishing the courses in clinical research, students can work with Biotech Companies, Pharma Industries, Labs, and Research firms.

Q. What are the job roles offered to students after finishing the courses in Clinical Research?
A. Some of the job roles in clinical research field have been mentioned below:

Clinical Research Managers (CRM)
Biostatistician
Clinical Research Coordinator (CRO)
Lecturer
Project Manager
Counselors

Q. What is the average salary of a graduate in Clinical Research?
A. The average salary of a graduate in clinical Research is INR 2 lakh to 4 lakh. The salary increases as per the experience and skill.
