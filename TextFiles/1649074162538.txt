Edy








Constitutional law is termed as the supreme law in the Indian Judicial system. It comprises laws related to both the government and its people. It is a law body consisting of a set of powers and roles. Constitutional law deals with the functioning of ruling bodies and their relationship with each other. It includes the structure of different entities like the parliament and the executive within a state. Constitutional law also comprises the basic rights of citizens. There is a vast scope for aspirants in this field and one having a keen desire in the Indian Constitutional system and its functioning can opt for this field.
Constitutional Law has become one of the most popular specialisations and here we have discussed multiple aspects of this law field.

Constitutional Law Syllabus and Subjects
Both undergraduate and postgraduate Constitutional Law courses cover different aspects of the field. Given below is detailed elaboration on Constitutional Law subjects taught in different colleges and universities:
Constitutional Law Syllabus at Undergraduate Level:
At UG level, candidates are usually not given an option to choose Constitutional Law as a specialisation. However, given below are some common Constitutional Law subjects that are taught in a UG course:


Delegated Legislation

Scope, Significance and Trends in Administrative Law

Administrative Adjudication

Principles of Natural Justice

Statutory Provisions of Natural Justice

Important FAQs related to Constitutional Law

Q: What is the meaning of constitutional law?
A: Constitutional law is about bond between the individual and the state, as per the law. It also deals with political relations within a society.

Q: What is difference between law and constitution?


A: The Constitution of any country is the supreme law of the land whereas law is defined as a set of norms and rules to govern society and governmental institutions.

Q: What are the popular traits needed for a Constitutional lawyer?

A: A constitutional lawyer should have excellent communication skills, good analytical skills, problem solving, positive approach and many other similar skills.

Q: What is the maximum duration of a law course?


A: If a candidate choose LLB course after 12
th

standard, it is of five years. Meanwhile, if you opt LLB after graduation, it is of three years duration.

Q: Can Constitutional Law course be done online?

A: This course is offered by many prestigious colleges and institutes in offline mode. However, many universities offer Constitutional Law diploma courses in online mode.
