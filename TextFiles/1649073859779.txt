What is NMAT?
NMAT or NMAT by GMAC is a national level entrance test conducted for admissions at NMIMS University and other reputed B-Schools in India as well as abroad. conducted NMAT until GMAC took over the authority. NMAT is designed to keep the test-takers convenience in mind. Candidates can choose the exam date and venue preference during the exam window using the NMAT scheduling option. NMAT shares some features with as candidates are allowed to choose the preferred date during a testing window as well as self-schedule the test, time and venue, appointment date based on the availability of seats at the preferred test centre. The national level entrance exam, NMAT, is conducted once a year during the test window of specified days. NMAT by GMAC score is based on the performance of candidates in three sections, namely, Language Skills, Quantitative Skills and Logical Reasoning.

FAQs on NMAT Exam 2022

Q: How many times is NMAT conducted?
A: Generally NMAT is conducted once a year, but owing to the COVID-19 situation, last year the second phase of NMAT was conducted in January.

Q: Who is eligible to take the second phase of NMAT 2022?
A: In case the second phase of the exam is conducted, candidates who had not taken the exam or not taken all three attempts are eligible to apply for the second phase of NMAT.

Q: What is the test window in NMAT?
A: NMAT is a unique MBA exam, having a 70 to 90-day testing window. For NMAT 2022, GMAC will conduct exam under a 74-day window. This provision is included to cut the stress level of the exam takers. Candidates can schedule the test as per convenience and individual preparation level. Candidates are also allowed to take NMAT by GMAC exam for a maximum of three attempts. In case, the candidates are not satisfied with their performance or if they are not confident about their preparation level, they can use the advantage of three attempts in NMAT. Test takers can book test slots as per convenience and readiness to give the test.

Q: How many times can one register for NMAT?
A: Registration for the NMAT exam is accepted one time. However, candidates can take the test twice, for which they have to reschedule the test.

Q: Is there a form correction facility available for the NMAT application process?
A: No, there is no provision of form correction in the NMAT exam. The 'School Preference' and 'Communication Preference' fields in the application form is enabled after the submission of the application fee. All the particulars must be carefully cross-checked before submitting the NMAT application form.

Q: How can applicants know about the time slot and date of NMAT 2022?
A: The date, time slot and exam venue will be printed on the NMAT admit card. It is a chain of the process; first, the candidate will have to schedule the test using the 'schedule test' option available under the exam tab on the candidate dashboard in NMAT by GMAC account. The test scheduling option will be available after they have received a confirmation email from the exam conducting authority about the receipt of the NMAT application fee. Candidates can then choose the date and time slot of the test as per their convenience for NMAT by GMAC exam. The facility to book date and time slot along with the selection of preferable locations of the test centre is available on a first-come-first-serve basis.

Q: What is the procedure to retake NMAT?
A: Retake option for the NMAT exam is available in the candidate's existing registration account after the first attempt. The option to retake the test is present in the 'Retake' tab in the dashboard of the candidate’ account. Candidates have to wait 15 days before retaking the NMAT exam again. It is subject to payment of the application fee. Candidates can retake the NMAT exam twice during the 74-day test window.

Q: What is the procedure to reschedule the NMAT exam?
A: The facility to reschedule the NMAT exam is available 72 hours before the scheduled time slot. However, the facility to reschedule the exam on a different date, time and test venue is subject to availability as it is on first-cum-first serve basis. The additional fee has to be remitted via credit card only. A confirmation email regarding the approval of the rescheduled test slot will be sent to the candidate's registered email ID.

Q: Is there any option to review answers in the NMAT exam?
A: Yes, there is an option to review answers in the NMAT exam. Candidates can review as well as make changes in the answers in different sections only within the slated sectional time limit. There is no option to move back and forth between sections. If a test taker moves to the next section, they will not be able to make any changes in the previous section.

Q: What do scaled and percentile scores refer to in the NMAT exam?
A: Here is the basic difference between scaled scores and percentile scores in NMAT:

Scaled scores: The test forms are equated to account for any difference in the overall difficulty and the raw test scores are normalized on a common scale to arrive at scaled scores.
Percentile scores: The percentile rank or score is an indicator of the candidate’s score/rank as compared to the score/rank of all the other test-takers.

Q: How long does the NMAT exam window remain open?
A: The NMAT exam is conducted over a three-month period. It is divided into five windows in which each window is 15-day long (the first and the last date is inclusive in the window).

Q: What is the meaning of retake of the NMAT exam?
A: The meaning of retake of the NMAT exam implies that any applicant can attempt the test thrice including the first attempt. In other words, the process of reapplying for the exam is known as exam retake. However, candidates can reappear for the exam, not before 15 days from the first attempt.

Q: What is the scheduling and rescheduling of the NMAT exam?
A: Scheduling the NMAT exam refers to choosing a time slot for the test. Aspirants can schedule the test by paying the registration fee. Candidates can, however, 'reschedule' the date if they due to some unforeseen reason are not able to appear for the test on the allotted date. There is another provision available in NMAT, known as re-take scheduling. It implies, that candidates can re-take the exam if they wish to by applying the re-take exam schedule option.

Q: Is the NMAT exam tough?
A: In comparison with any other MBA entrance exams, the level of difficulty of the NMAT exam is moderate.

Q: What is the difference between the NMAT exam and other MBA entrance exams?
A: Unlike other MBA entrance exams, the NMAT exam is held over a period of approximately 90 days and an examinee can appear for the test three times including the first attempt in the same year to improve their score. This year, an additional phase of the exam was conducted from February 2 to February 8.
