Diploma is a type of recognition issued by an educational institution to a person on the completion of an educational course successfully. It is a short-term course of 1 to 2 years usually awarded for professional/vocational courses.
After completion of the Distance/Correspondence Diploma, aspirants can apply for admission to regular or distance degree programmes. Aspirants holding a Diploma in engineering can apply for admission to BE/BTech, admission to BE/BTech programme is done through and various state-level engineering entrance examinations. However, some institutes also conduct their entrance exam to provide admission to eligible students. Similarly, students possessing a Diploma in the medical field can apply for admission to BPharma, BSc, MBBS, etc. is conducted every year for admission to MBBS whereas there are various state level entrance examinations for admission to BPharma and BSc.

Distance/Correspondence Diploma courses in India Eligibility Criteria
For admission in Diploma courses in India candidates must have passed 10+2. While pursuing a diploma, a person is trained in a particular field. It is not necessary that the institute or the university offering diploma education be UGC or AICTE approved. It can be studied full-time or from distance/correspondence.
